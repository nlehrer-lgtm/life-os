## Quick Summary

Messenger Cup is Messenger International's largest annual event — a 4-day, high-touch hospitality and fundraising experience at the Broadmoor Hotel and Resort in Colorado Springs, CO. ~300 business leaders attend. The event raises approximately **$4 million** annually for global resource distribution and translation.

---

## Event Structure

- **Duration:** 4 days
- **Venue:** The Broadmoor Hotel and Resort, Colorado Springs, CO
- **Audience:** ~300 business leaders (men and women)
- **Activities:** Golf, alpine activities, spa, dinners, themed experiences, speaking events, awards/tournaments
- **Fundraiser night:** The emotional and operational center of the event — the main $4M raise

---

## Strategic Purpose

1. Relationship cultivation with high-capacity partners
2. Mission communication via storytelling, testimony, and presentation
3. Fundraising through a high-production live experience
4. Stewardship reinforcement — projecting excellence and trustworthiness
5. Post-event momentum via recap and follow-up media

## Planning Rhythm & Risk

The event has a **long runway with a volatile final phase.** Late-stage changes in the final week before the event are a recurring reality, even when concepts are signed off months in advance. Build margin into schedule, staffing, and file management. Never assume creative sign-off is final until very close to show.

### Known Risk Categories
- ProPresenter failure or signal/connection loss
- Playback issues (media formatting, routing, system instability)
- Late content changes pressuring QA and export timelines
- Venue-specific audio/screen/vendor issues
- Operational overload (running show + capturing content simultaneously)
