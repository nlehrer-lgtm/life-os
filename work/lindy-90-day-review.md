**Reviewer:** Nathaniel Lehrer, Director of Audio & Visual

**Overall Rating:** 8–9 / 10

## Overview

Lindy Wood stepped into the Production Manager role on January 5, 2026 — a significant transition from executor and editor to systems leader and team developer. This review covers her first 90 days in that role. Overall, she is the right person for this position. She has stepped up meaningfully from what she was previously doing, and her diligence has directly freed up her supervisor to invest time in new vision and team-level opportunities. The team respects and follows her. She strives for excellence in everything delivered. With a little more room to grow into the full weight of the role, she is tracking as a strong fit.

---

## Role Integration

Lindy is just now fully finding her footing — and that timing makes sense. The primary reason is that much of her first 90 days was consumed by onboarding LeVann, the team's newest hire, who is her direct report. That was the right call. He came from Premiere and had no DaVinci Resolve experience; she put the right curriculum and tools in his hands and has been patient and intentional through his adaptation. The investment is paying off: as LeVann becomes more self-sufficient, Lindy is visibly stepping more into the full scope of the Production Manager role — managing schedules, running meetings, setting plans, and keeping the team contextualized.

## Strengths & Wins

### 1. LeVann Onboarding
Lindy has done an excellent job making LeVann feel welcomed and equipped. She identified the DaVinci Resolve gap immediately and built a learning path for him. She has been patient with his transition and is bringing out the best in him — not just managing him, but developing him. This is a genuine win for a first-time people manager.

### 2. Quality Control & Decision-Making Authority
Lindy has confidently taken on QC decision-making without needing to defer to her supervisor. She comes with a question when genuinely unsure, but there has been no pattern of unnecessary escalation or micromanagement strain. She is functioning as the final technical voice on production output, which is exactly the goal for this role.

### 3. Communication Preparation & Context-Setting
In this area, Lindy has blown past expectations. She is consistently preparing the team with the documents, run sheets, timelines, and context they need before projects begin. The individual and role-based run sheets she developed are a real operational upgrade. Over 90 days, this growth has been notable and consistent.

### 4. Leading Up — Proactivity & Forward Vision
Lindy is always looking ahead. There are rarely things that fall through the cracks or get swept under the rug. She surfaces issues before they become problems, flags needs before being asked, and keeps her supervisor informed without requiring follow-up. She is reliable and visionary in this area.

### 5. MCUP Planning
One of the known lessons from the previous year was the need to start MCUP planning earlier and bring key stakeholders in sooner. Lindy was proactive in initiating those conversations this cycle without being asked. That's exactly the kind of ownership this role requires.

### 6. Coaching & Team Development
Perhaps the most encouraging surprise of the first 90 days: Lindy genuinely lights up in her one-on-ones with Josh, Annie, and LeVann. She is discovering that she loves the teaching and coaching side of leadership — and it shows. She has been thoughtful in understanding how each team member works, what motivates them, and where they need to grow. That natural energy is a real asset.

## Growth Edges

### 1. Communication That Lands
While Lindy has grown significantly in *preparing* communication, there is still development needed in ensuring that communication actually *sticks* with the people receiving it. There have been instances where information shared seems to fall on deaf ears. Whether this is a system issue, a posture issue, or a team issue, it's worth exploring together. The next step is not just sending the message — it's confirming it was received and retained.

### 2. Delegating More to Josh
Lindy is building good relational trust with Josh and understands how he works. The growth edge now is actually handing him more — even tasks she could easily do herself. Josh sometimes doesn't have enough work, and the opportunity is for Lindy to trust him with more ownership. This will free her up and develop him simultaneously.

### 3. QC System Documentation
The formal QC/SOP document — defining standards for color, audio, pacing, and branding — remains the highest-leverage unfinished initiative. It was appropriately deprioritized during LeVann's onboarding, and that tradeoff was reasonable. Now that he is more settled, this is the next major deliverable. The mental model is there. The ChatGPT draft exists. It needs to move from idea to document.

---

## Goals for the Next 90 Days

1. **Complete a draft of the QC/SOP document** — at minimum, a working version covering audio levels, color standards, pacing, and branding that the team can reference.
2. **Develop a delegation rhythm with Josh** — identify at least one recurring task or project type to hand off to him with clear expectations and a feedback loop.
3. **Experiment with communication confirmation systems** — find one new approach (a follow-up check-in, a written summary, a Slack thread recap) to ensure team communication isn't just sent but received.
