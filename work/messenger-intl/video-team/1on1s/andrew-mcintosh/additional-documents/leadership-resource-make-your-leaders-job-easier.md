## The Framework

Here's the context first, then the three tools.

### My job, in four buckets

When you're thinking about how to make my job easier, picture what actually fills my day:

1. **Problem solving** — fielding the constant stream of "hey Nathaniel, what about…"
2. **Coordination & planning** — timelines, sequencing, who's doing what when
3. **Approvals** — anything that comes out of the [[Video Team]] ultimately lands on my shoulders. My job is clarity, not just QC.
4. **Strategic vision** — looking ahead: where's the studio in a year, what happens at [[Messenger Cup]] season, where will the team burn out, where do we grow.

Every minute you can pull off buckets #1 and #2 is a minute I can spend on #4. That's how the team levels up.

The three tools below are all aimed at that same target.

---

### Tool 1 — Recommendations, Not Questions

**The shift:** When you bring something to me, lead with a recommendation, not an open-ended question.

**What this is NOT:** "Stop asking questions." Questions are great — *especially up front*. Ask a million questions early to get crystal clear, so you don't have to ask later. Questions to me up the chain and to [[Josh]] / [[LeVann]] down the chain are how clarity gets built.

**What it IS:** When the moment comes to bring a decision to me, don't hand me the open problem. Hand me your discernment, plus the path you're leaning toward, plus the reasoning.

#### Compare

| ❌ Question mode | ✅ Recommendation mode |
| --- | --- |
| "Hey Nathaniel, what should this set look like for the next podcast?" | "Hey Nathaniel — I've got two directions for this set. I'm leaning Option A because the colors match the talent's vibe and the depth works with the lighting grid. Option B is the safer call if we want continuity with last episode. Do you approve A, or should I keep exploring by end of day?" |
| Forces me into problem-solving + coordination | Lets me sit in **approval** mode → done in 30 seconds |
| My brain has to start from zero | Your Discernment + Invention have already done the work |

#### Why this is *your* superpower

You have **Discernment** and **Invention** as Working Geniuses. That means you are *literally wired* to:

- Look at two options and know which is better (Discernment)
- Generate the options in the first place (Invention)

I don't have Discernment as a genius. When questions get dumped on me without a recommendation, I have to manufacture the answer cold — and honestly, that's one of the things that drains me as a leader. The assumption is "I have authority, so I should decide." But you're a smart person; most of the time you already know the answer better than I do. **Trust your discernment and bring it forward.**

#### The format

> "Hey Nathaniel — here are 2 options. I'm leaning **[Option]** because **[reasoning]**. Do you approve, or should I keep exploring [by when]?"

### Tool 2 — Tuesday, Not Friday

**The shift:** The moment you see a timeline slip, a complexity, or a blocker — surface it. Don't wait for the end of the week. And come with a proposed path forward, not just the problem.

**The clarification:** It's not literally about days of the week. It's about *the second you see something is going to move*. The sooner I know, the more degrees of freedom I have to help. The later I know, the fewer options either of us has.

#### Compare

| ❌ Friday afternoon | ✅ Tuesday morning |
| --- | --- |
| "Hey, I couldn't get to John's new set this week — Unreal was acting up all week." | "Hey, Unreal is being a pain today. I can see this eating most of the week. My take: I should prioritize fixing Unreal and push John's set to next week — that keeps us clear for the [[Messenger Cup]] shoot. Want me to go that way, or hold the John timeline?" |
| Now I'm scrambling, talking to Lisa, rearranging the whole team's week | I make one call, you keep moving, nobody's surprised |
| You worked hard all week and the situation got worse | Same hard work, but the team adapted in real time |

#### The follow-up you asked about

You asked: *"If I tell you 4 days, then next day it changes to 2 days, then later to 4-5 days again, isn't that more coordination noise for you?"*

Great question. The answer: **don't ping me with every micro-update.** The principle is:

> "I know the situation better than Nathaniel does right now. Let me think through how this affects everything else we've discussed, and then bring him my recommended path forward."

So this point links straight back to Tool 1. Tuesday-not-Friday is *when* you communicate; Recommendations-not-Questions is *how*. Together: **"Here's what shifted, here's how I think we should adjust, do you approve?"**

#### The one-liner

> **"Run ahead. I'll catch up."**

You don't need to wait for me to bless every next step. Use the information from [[Get REC'D]], from 1:1s, from launch meetings, and *run*. Get ahead. When we meet, you're presenting me with thinking I can approve and strategize against — not raw material I have to process.

### Tool 3 — Ship-Ready, Not Fresh-Eyes

**The shift:** When you send me something for review, send it like you're shipping it. Not like you're asking me to find what's wrong.

#### Two modes of sending work

| ❌ Fresh-eyes mode (~90%) | ✅ Ship-ready mode (~99%) |
| --- | --- |
| "Hey, take a look — anything jump out?" | "Here's v1. I made the call on X because Y. The one thing I want your eye on is Z." |
| Pulls me into problem-solving | Lets me stay in approval / strategic vision |
| Implicitly says: *help me discern this* | Says: *I've already discerned this — confirm I'm aligned* |
| Sometimes triggers me to nitpick things you'd already considered | Lets me trust your judgment and move fast |

#### Why this matters — the trust flywheel

I'll be honest with you about how this works on my end. [[Lindy]] has earned this on color grading. I rarely leave notes anymore because she just *doesn't miss*. So when I do see something, my reaction is, "Huh — that's not like her. Did something happen, or was that on purpose?" And we can have a 10-second conversation instead of a notes session.

That's what I want with you on the things you own. The more often you ship work at 99%-ready with the rationale baked in, the more I default to **"Andrew thought about this. There's a reason. Approved."** Which means:

- Faster approvals
- Fewer rounds
- I bow out of areas you own
- You operate with real authority, not just title

#### What about the "keep me in the dark" question?

You asked something really sharp: *how do you flag something you're unsure about without priming me to see the flaw?* (The AI hand, the wonky VFX, etc.)

My answer: **I'm okay being in the dark on a lot more than you think.** If you've made a judgment call — even on "this isn't perfect, but fixing it costs a whole day and isn't worth it" — bring me the call *with the reasoning*, not the worry.

> "Hey, the hand in this AI shot is a little off. To re-render is a full day. My call is to ship it because [reason]. Flagging it so you know — but I'm going to move on unless you push back."

That's discernment in action. That's leadership. That's not "did you notice this?" — that's "I noticed this, here's what I'm doing about it."

The principle:

> Every piece of work you send me should carry your **decision** and your **reasoning** — not your **uncertainty**.

## How the three tools work together

They aren't three separate things. They're one posture, three angles:

```
Recommendations, not Questions  →  HOW you communicate
Tuesday, not Friday             →  WHEN you communicate
Ship-Ready, not Fresh-Eyes      →  WHAT you communicate
```

All three:
- Lean on your **Discernment + Invention** (your geniuses)
- Pull me out of problem-solving / coordination and into approval / strategy
- Build the trust flywheel that lets you take more ownership
