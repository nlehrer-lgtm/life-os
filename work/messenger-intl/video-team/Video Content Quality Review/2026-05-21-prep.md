# Video Content Quality Review — Prep (2026-05-21, 10:30–11:15am)

With [[Andrew McIntosh]] and [[Josh Kendrick]] — production team leads on the [[Video Team]].

## Goal

Honest, technical, no-ego audit of the last year of video output across deliverable types — podcasts, one-off shoots, MXDaily, [[Messenger Cup|MCUP]] content, social. Land on a short list of things to **stop**, **keep**, and **learn** before we plan the next quarter of shoots.

## How to run this

- **Andrew's wheelhouse** — Discernment + Invention. Let him drive the "what's actually working / what should this be" calls. Ask him direct evaluative questions, not open ponder-ones.
- **Josh's wheelhouse** — execution on set. He's the one holding the camera and rigging lights, so he sees the problems we don't see in dailies. Pull on-the-floor observations from him.
- Hold to 45 min. Use the three-bucket frame (Subpar / Doing well / Need to learn) for every topic — don't let one topic eat the whole meeting.
- **Decisions, not just discussion.** End each topic with either (a) a standard we're committing to, (b) an experiment to run on the next shoot, or (c) a parking-lot note for a deeper session.

## Topic 1 — Lighting

**Subpar / risk questions**
- Are we consistent shoot-to-shoot, or does every set look slightly different?
- Mixed color temperatures — are we still fighting tungsten/daylight clashes with practicals + key lights?
- Glasses glare, hot spots, blown highlights on foreheads — how often is this showing up in dailies?
- Negative fill — are we using it, or are faces still flat from too much ambient bounce?
- How does our lighting interact with the [[LED wall|LED wall backgrounds]]? Spill onto subject? Wall washing out the talent?

**Doing well — call out**
- Where has the lighting genuinely elevated content this year? (Specific shoots — name them.)

**Learn / improve**
- Should we standardize a baseline lighting setup per format (podcast, MXDaily, interview) so we're not redesigning every shoot from scratch?
- Modifier inventory — what's missing? (Soft boxes, flags, scrims, hair lights.)
- Do we need a lighting reference doc / "house style" for shooters to default to?

## Topic 2 — Camera Settings

**Subpar / risk questions**
- Resolution + frame rate — are we locked across multi-cam, or do we have mismatched files hitting the edit?
- Picture profile — LOG everywhere, or are some shoots ending up Rec.709 baked-in?
- Shutter discipline — 180° rule holding, or are we seeing weird motion across cuts?
- ISO ceiling — what's our actual usable ISO on our current bodies before noise becomes a problem?
- White balance — fixed Kelvin or auto? How often does WB drift bite us in the edit?
- Multi-cam matching — do our cameras actually match, or is Andrew color-correcting that gap in post every time?

**Doing well — call out**
- Where do our settings show up as "looks expensive"? Where do they show up as "looks like a YouTube video"?

**Learn / improve**
- Lock a camera settings sheet per shoot type. Print it. Put it in the kit.
- Lens choices — are we leaning on the same 2–3 lenses? Anything underused or missing?
- Focus discipline — autofocus reliability vs. pulling focus manually. What's our standard?

## Topic 3 — LED Wall Backgrounds

**Subpar / risk questions**
- Moiré — still showing up? On which camera/lens combos?
- Color spill on talent — are we accounting for it in the key, or losing skin tones?
- Brightness — wall hot enough to compete with subject? Talent in silhouette?
- Refresh rate / sync — are we still getting scan lines, or have we dialed that in?
- Content on the wall — are we using it intentionally (story, brand) or as default wallpaper?
- Distance from talent — close enough to wrap, far enough to avoid spill?

**Doing well — call out**
- Best use of the wall this year — what's the highlight reel moment?

**Learn / improve**
- Build a "tested combinations" list: camera + lens + wall content + distance that we *know* works. Anything outside that list = test first.
- Inventory the wall's health: dead pixels, panel issues, calibration drift.
- A library of wall-friendly background content so we're not designing one each shoot.

## Topic 4 — Color Grading

**Subpar / risk questions**
- Are we using a unified base LUT, or is every project starting from scratch?
- Skin tone consistency — does the same talent look the same across MXDaily, podcast, and one-off shoots?
- Brand color palette — is there one, and does the grade honor it?
- Match grading across cameras within a project — clean, or always painful?
- Delivery — Rec.709 standard? Any HDR delivery on the horizon we should be prepping for?

**Doing well — call out**
- Where has the grade carried a piece?

**Learn / improve**
- Establish a house LUT / show LUT per format.
- Color-managed timeline in Resolve — are we set up for it?
- A grading reference frame for each major series so we're not re-deciding the look every episode.

## Topic 5 — Adjacent items (if time)

Quick passes — flag anything urgent, otherwise park for a follow-up.
- **Audio** — mic discipline, room treatment, post sweetening. Easy to neglect; ruins video faster than bad picture.
- **Workflow / media management** — card offload, backups, file naming, project handoff to edit.
- **Post pipeline** — turnaround time, review loops, where bottlenecks live.

## Decisions to leave the room with

By 11:10 we should have, on paper:
1. **Stop doing** — 2–3 specific things we're cutting.
2. **Keep doing** — 2–3 things we're explicitly committing to keep as standard.
3. **Learn / experiment** — 2–3 things we'll test on the next shoot.
4. **Owner + next step** for each — including who's drafting the per-format settings sheets and house LUT if we go that direction.

## Questions to open with

- "If I gave you the last year of our content and asked you to pick the three weakest pieces — what's on that list and why?"
- "What's the one technical thing on set you wish we'd fix and haven't?"
- "What's a piece this year you'd show someone if you wanted to make us look great?"
