# Video Team Identity

_The full identity of the [[Video Team]] at [[Messenger International]] — who we are, what we exist to do, who we serve, what we uniquely own, and the pillars we hold each other to. Living doc. Revisit annually as a team._

> **Note on pillars and silos:** This document holds the [[Video Team]]'s own identity — including our **REC'D** pillars. The ministry-level [[Messenger International]] pillars are a separate framework and live in their own home; we don't conflate them. Our pillars describe how *we* operate as a team; MI's pillars describe how the ministry as a whole operates.

---

## 1. Who we are — the short version

The **[[Video Team]]** at [[Messenger International]] is the team that turns the ministry's vision into footage that honors what makes it powerful. We are the lane that makes sure a teaching, a story, or a moment doesn't lose its weight on the way to a screen. We capture faithfully.

Inside the [[Discipleship Team]]'s 3 C's framework (Coordinate / Create / Capture), we are **Capture**.

---

## 2. Our mission — two versions

### A. General-audience version

_Use this when introducing the team to anyone outside the [[Discipleship Team]] — a new partner, vendor, board member, or staff member who doesn't know what the 3 C's are._

The [[Video Team]] at [[Messenger International]] exists to turn the ministry's vision into footage that honors what makes it powerful. When the leaders of [[Messenger International|Messenger]] have a teaching, a story, or a moment they want the world to receive, we are the team that translates it into something people can actually watch — without losing the weight of what was said.

What we uniquely own is the **craft of how [[Messenger International|Messenger]]'s content looks and sounds.** Cinematography, editing, sound design, color, deliverable quality. The standard the ministry's content meets is on us.

Full doc: [[Role and Mission General|role-and-mission-general.md]].

### B. Inside the 3 C's (Discipleship Team framing)

_Use this inside the [[Discipleship Team|Touch Base Huddle]] and any conversation with [[Arden]], Adrian, [[Chris Pace|Chris]], or anyone who works in the Coordinate / Create / Capture model._

The [[Video Team]] exists to be the trusted hands that turn the ministry's vision into footage that honors what makes it powerful. We capture faithfully.

Inside the 3 C's:

- **Coordinate** decides *what* gets made and when ([[Arden]] / Adrian).
- **Create** decides *how it's said* and how it's packaged ([[Chris Pace|Chris]]).
- **Capture** decides *whether it's worth watching* (us).

The 3 C's only work if each lane stays in its lane and passes the baton cleanly. Capture isn't where ideas originate. It isn't where final packaging decisions get made. It's where the vision becomes a finished thing — and where the *quality* of that finished thing lives or dies.

Full doc: [[Role and Mission|role-and-mission.md]].

## 4. What we uniquely own — the craft of how it looks and sounds

This is the [[Video Team]]'s lane. Nobody else at [[Messenger International]] owns this.

- Cinematography
- Editing
- Sound design
- Color
- Deliverable quality
- The aesthetic and technical standard the ministry's content is held to

The standard our content meets — visually, sonically, emotionally — is on us. When [[Messenger International]] sends out a video, the production quality has to match the weight of what's being said. If the craft is weak, the message is undercut.

## 5. Our pillars — REC'D

_The four pillars the [[Video Team]] holds itself to. We revisit these each year together and remind ourselves what we stand for. The weekly [[Get REC'D]] meeting is the rhythm that keeps them alive._

| Letter | Pillar | What it means for us |
|---|---|---|
| **R** | **Resilience** | We don't fold under tight timelines, shifting creative direction, or technical chaos. We absorb pressure and keep delivering. |
| **E** | **Excellence** | The craft is the product. We hold a high bar on cinematography, edit, sound, color, and finish — every single deliverable. |
| **C** | **Communication** | We don't let things go to red. We loop people in early, give status before being asked, name problems plainly, and pass the baton cleanly across lanes. |
| **D** | **Discipline** | We show up on time. We hit deadlines. We keep our standards in the small things (no open laptops in [[Get REC'D]], full attention to the speaker) because the small things compound. |

These are how we hold each other accountable. They're also the lens for hiring, feedback, and growth conversations on the team.

> **REC'D is the Video Team's own framework.** It does not replace and is not derived from any ministry-wide pillars [[Messenger International]] may articulate at the organization level. They live in different silos by design — see the note at the top of this doc.

## 8. Our anchor rhythm — [[Get REC'D]]

**Tuesdays at 8:00 AM.** Always.

The [[Get REC'D]] meeting is the [[Video Team]]'s weekly all-hands and the most important recurring meeting on the team's calendar. It's the one meeting each week with enough room to talk **vision and calendar together** and keep the REC'D pillars alive in how we work.

Format:

1. **Icebreaker / opener** — rotating team member (alphabetical), their slot
2. **Updates** — [[Lindy Wood|Lindy]] walks the calendar and the week
3. **Team-wide updates / vision** — Nathaniel closes with news, vision casting, encouragement

Standards (these reflect the **Discipline** pillar — non-negotiable):

- Be on time. No exceptions.
- No open laptops. Only the current speaker has a screen open. Everyone else gives full attention.

Full meeting context: [[Get REC'D|Get REC'D Meeting/CONTEXT.md]].
