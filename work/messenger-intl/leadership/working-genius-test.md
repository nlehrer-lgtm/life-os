# Working Genius — Self-Assessment

A homemade version of Patrick Lencioni's *6 Types of Working Genius* assessment. Built from the publicly described model; not affiliated with The Table Group.

## How it works

There are 36 statements below. For each one, rate how true it is of you on a scale of **1 to 5**:

- **1** — Strongly disagree. Not me at all. This kind of thing drains me.
- **2** — Disagree. Mostly not me.
- **3** — Neutral. Sometimes, depending on the situation.
- **4** — Agree. Mostly me.
- **5** — Strongly agree. Very much me. This kind of thing gives me energy and joy.

**A few notes before you start:**

- "Work" in this test doesn't mean a job. It means anything you put energy into — raising kids, running the home, parenting, friendships, hosting, planning, making things, helping people, decisions you're part of with Nathaniel. Answer for *all of life*, not just paid work.
- If a statement mentions an example that doesn't quite fit your life, just focus on the **feeling** of it. Do you generally do this kind of thing, in any form? Trust your gut.
- Answer based on what **energizes** you and feels natural — not what you've gotten good at doing because you had to.
- Don't overthink any one statement. The test takes about 8–12 minutes.

**Don't peek at the scoring key at the bottom of this file until you're done answering.**

## The Statements

1. I often find myself thinking, "There has to be a better way to do this," about everyday things — how we run the house, our routines, how we do meals or bedtime.
2. When a friend or family member is stuck on a problem, my brain lights up and I love coming up with fresh ideas for them to try.
3. When someone shares an idea or plan with me, I usually have a strong gut feeling about whether it'll actually work — even if I can't fully explain why.
4. I love getting people excited about an idea and pulling them into doing something together.
5. I get real satisfaction from quietly providing what Nathaniel, the kids, or a friend needs — without needing any credit for it.
6. I get a real sense of satisfaction from finishing what I started — whether it's laundry, a project, or a goal — all the way to done.
7. I'm the kind of person who asks "why do we even do it this way?" about things most people just accept.
8. I get a rush of energy from starting something brand new — a meal I've never made, a craft, a way of decorating a room — without a recipe or guide.
9. People come to me for a second opinion because I can sense when something is off, even when everyone else thinks it looks fine.
10. I'm comfortable being the one who says "okay, let's actually do this!" and gets the family or a group moving.
11. When I see someone struggling — one of the kids, a friend, a fellow mom — my first instinct is to step in and help, even without being asked.
12. I'm comfortable pushing through tiredness, interruptions, or obstacles to make sure something actually gets finished.
13. I love sitting with big questions about life, parenting, faith, or marriage — even when there's nothing I need to decide right now.
14. Friends or family come to me when they're stuck and need a creative idea they haven't thought of yet.
15. When someone shares an idea with me, I'm good at helping them keep what's working and reshape what isn't.
16. I get energy from convincing someone — Nathaniel, a friend, the kids — to try something new or commit to a plan.
17. I find joy in being the steady, supportive presence behind other people's wins — happy when *they* shine.
18. I'd rather finish a project I've already started than abandon it and begin something new.
19. I tend to notice when something feels "off" in our home, with the kids, or in a friendship before anyone else does — even if I can't say exactly what's wrong yet.
20. I'd rather come up with my own way of doing something than follow a tutorial, a recipe, or someone else's playbook.
21. I can usually tell pretty quickly when something feels right or feels off — about a person, a situation, a decision — and I trust that instinct.
22. When I'm excited about something, people around me tend to catch that excitement and follow my lead.
23. I'm comfortable rearranging my own plans or day to support what Nathaniel or the kids are working on.
24. I'm willing to do the boring, unglamorous parts — the small steps, the cleanup, the follow-through — to make sure something gets fully done.
25. When our routines have been the same for a long stretch, I start feeling restless and wanting to shake things up.
26. I feel most alive when I'm dreaming up possibilities — even ideas I know might not actually happen.
27. I enjoy weighing options and helping decide which path makes the most sense — whether it's a big family decision or just where to go for dinner.
28. I enjoy being the nudge that gets someone off the fence and into action.
29. I'd rather help bring someone else's vision to life than push for my own way.
30. Unfinished things — a half-decorated room, a project that stalled out, a conversation that never got resolved — really bother me. I want to get them to "done."
31. I enjoy thinking long and slow about questions that don't have a clear answer — turning them over in my mind for days.
32. I'm comfortable suggesting ideas that sound a little wild or unusual at first — for a family adventure, a new tradition, a project around the house.
33. I tend to spot patterns or inconsistencies that other people miss — in someone's behavior, in a story, or in a situation.
34. When something matters to me, I'm willing to keep bringing it up until the people around me get on board.
35. People often tell me I'm the kind of person who makes them feel seen, supported, and cared for.
36. I take pride in actually following through on what I said I'd do — not just talking or dreaming about it.

## Scoring Key

## How to interpret your scores

- Your **two highest scores** are your **Working Geniuses** — where you thrive and feel alive.
- Your **two lowest scores** are your **Working Frustrations** — activities that drain you, even if you can do them.
- The **two in the middle** are your **Working Competencies** — you can do them fine, but they don't fill your tank.

Ties are normal. If two scores are within 1–2 points of each other, the order may not be meaningful — look at how the activities actually feel, not the exact number.

## The six geniuses, in plain English

- **Wonder** — identifies the need for change. Pondering, observing, asking "what if?"
- **Invention** — generates novel ideas and solutions from scratch. The blank-page creator.
- **Discernment** — evaluates whether an idea is sound. Gut-check, pattern recognition, refining.
- **Galvanizing** — rallies people and builds momentum. The "let's go!" energy.
- **Enablement** — supports and assists others. The human glue, the responsive helper.
- **Tenacity** — pushes through to completion. Finishes what was started.

The geniuses flow in this order (WIDGET): Wonder → Invention → Discernment → Galvanizing → Enablement → Tenacity. Every project, decision, or initiative naturally moves through these phases, which is why families, teams, and groups need all six represented somewhere.

## Pairings

Your top two combined form your "pairing" — there are 15 total. After you have your two top geniuses, you can look up your pairing name (e.g., I+D = "The Discriminating Ideator," W+T = "The Cautious Implementer") in Lencioni's framework for more nuance.
