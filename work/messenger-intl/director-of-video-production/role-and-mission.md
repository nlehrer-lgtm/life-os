# Video Team Director — Role and Mission

_Why the [[Video Team]] exists at [[Messenger International]], who we serve, and what Capture uniquely owns inside the 3 C's. Written 2026-06-01 to bring into the [[Discipleship Team|Touch Base Huddle]]._

---

## Mission

**The [[Video Team]] exists to be the trusted hands that turn the ministry's vision into footage that honors what makes it powerful.** We capture faithfully. When someone — John, Lisa, [[Arden]], [[Chris Pace|Chris]] — has a teaching, a moment, or a story they want the world to receive, we are the lane that makes sure it doesn't lose its weight on the way to a screen.

That's the mission. Not "we make videos." We **steward vision into something watchable** without breaking it.

---

## Who We Serve — In Order

1. **The end viewer — globally.** The person, somewhere in the world, who is going to encounter [[Messenger International]] through a screen. Especially the one who would never otherwise hear what John and Lisa have to say. They are the ultimate aim. Everything we shoot points to them.
2. **The [[Beveres|Beveres]] — and the leaders we serve directly.** [[John Bevere|John]], Lisa, [[Arden]]. Our daily job is translating their voice and vision into something that scales beyond their physical presence. We are the multiplier on their reach.
3. **The [[Discipleship Team]] — our partners.** [[Arden]] and Adrian on Coordinate; [[Chris Pace|Chris]] on Create. Our job in the 3 C's is to make their work *actually shootable, finishable, and shippable.* They can't do their lanes if we don't do ours.

---

## What Capture Uniquely Owns

Inside the 3 C's, Capture owns the **craft of how it looks.**

- Cinematography
- Editing
- Sound design
- Color
- Deliverable quality
- The aesthetic and technical standard the ministry's content gets held to

Nobody else owns this. Coordinate decides what gets made and when. Create decides what it says and how it's packaged. **Capture decides whether it's worth watching.** The standard our content meets — visually, sonically, emotionally — is on us.

That's the line. Capture isn't where ideas originate. It isn't where final packaging decisions get made. It's where the vision becomes a finished thing — and where the *quality* of that finished thing lives or dies.

---

## My Role as Director

I lead the Capture lane. That means two things:

1. **Keep the craft at a level the ministry can stake its message on.** When [[Messenger International]] sends out a video, the production quality has to match the weight of what's being said. If the craft is weak, the message is undercut. My job is to make sure that never happens.
2. **Develop the team so the craft compounds.** [[Lindy Wood|Lindy]], [[Andrew McIntosh|Andrew]], [[Josh Kendrick|Josh]], [[Annie Duda|Annie]], [[LeVann Anthony Jr|LeVann]] — they execute the work. My job is to grow them so the standard gets sharper every year, not flatter. Our 2030 capacity comes from this — not from headcount, but from people who can hold the standard.
