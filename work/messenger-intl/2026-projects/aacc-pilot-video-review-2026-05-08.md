## Summary of Our Thoughts

The vendor clearly put real effort in and the content is high quality on the surface — sets are produced, talent is brought in, and lighting/audio show intent. But the technical execution is inconsistent enough that, as is, we wouldn't feel proud putting these on the Messenger X app. The biggest issues are skin tones, exposure inconsistencies, crushed blacks, and a lack of cohesion between sets, which together make the lessons feel jarring when watched as a course.

We don't feel it makes sense for our team to do meticulous correction work on delivered content — especially without raw files. The right path is to give AACC clear, constructive feedback on what needs to change moving forward, and only do light in-house cleanup (e.g., overall compressor pass, basic exposure/skin-tone tweaks) on these first 8 if Arden decides to use them. If the partnership is long-term, we'd suggest sending a couple of video team members (e.g., Nathaniel, Andrew, Lindy) out to workshop pre-production, production, and post with their team — that would scale better than us correcting every delivery.

If these pilot videos are used, we'd request the work happen **post-MCUP**. They'll require branding (intros, possibly lower thirds), audio cleanup, and color work that can't fit before MCUP delivery.

## What Needs to Change

### Visual / Color
- Skin tones across nearly all subjects are too yellow/warm.
- Background blue is unclear — varies in shade and reads strangely; subjects' edges look almost like a green screen against it.
- Three different sets, all exposed differently — feels jarring when watched together as a course.
- Blacks are crushed on multiple shots; no recoverable information in the dark areas.
- Exposure mismatched between two subjects on the same set (one face well-lit, the other in the 300s on false color).
- Practical lights inconsistent — on one shot, one subject's practical is on and the other's isn't.
- Highlights too harsh on close-ups, especially on the women — faces look overexposed and "splotchy."
- Lighting is flat and "blasting" rather than shaping — needs softer overall light and some backlight; harsh front-only light makes faces look wider and less flattering.
- Bookshelf set: wood tones are good, but the wall color is in the same palette as the bookshelf and feels visually busy.
- Reflection visible behind subjects (looks like a glass overlay on the shelf) is distracting.
- Too much skin-tone color on set (chairs, clothing, talent) — palette needs more variation.
- Red logo on a stand is visible in one shot and needs to be cropped or covered.
- Cord management on set is messy — should be cleaned up.

### Audio
- No high-pass filter applied — low rumble visible on the waveform.
- No EQ; unclear whether any compressor was used.
- Mastering inconsistent across speakers — female voices noticeably lower than male voices, requires constant volume adjustment.
- Some lines have rough cuts where the first words feel clipped off.
- Some moments drop super soft.
- Need a clear, consistent mastering process across all mics with an export-stage optimization pass.
- AI stem separation will struggle where speakers talk over each other, so raw isolated files would be the cleanest fix path.

### Framing / Composition
- Two-chair wide shots feel awkwardly spaced — chairs read as close together but the shot still cuts off ankles. Crop in tighter, or push the chairs in.
- Speaker positioning is inconsistent — sometimes host is camera-left, sometimes camera-right. Lock the host to one side across all lessons for cohesion.
- Only two cameras (teacher close-up + wide) — close-ups don't capture the other side of the face, and there's no coverage of the host during teacher-focused moments.
- Subjects are turned too far toward each other in profile, creating a disconnect from the audience — cheat both speakers slightly more toward camera.
- Eyeline mismatch between close-up and wide shots (eyeline reads higher in close-up, lower in wide).
- One shot has a subject visibly swiveling in his chair.

### Branding / Format / Cohesion
- No branding at the open — videos fade in cold; needs a Messenger-branded intro.
- Lower thirds likely needed.
- Set inconsistency across lessons makes the course feel disjointed — ideally consolidate to one set, or at minimum keep shooting/lighting/grade consistent across whichever sets are used.
- Open question for Arden: were these recorded together as a course, or are they separate teachings being packaged together? That answer changes how aggressively we push for set consolidation.
