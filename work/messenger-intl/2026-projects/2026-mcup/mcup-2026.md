## Creative Direction — Lisa Bevere

*From the April 8, 2026 "MCUP Opener Discussion" meeting*

### Theme & Scripture
- **Scripture anchor:** Revelation 3:8 — "I have placed before you an open door that no one can shut. I know that you have little strength, yet you have kept my word and not denied my name." (Church of Philadelphia)
- **Central idea:** America's prophetic mandate — not her greatness as a nation, but her role as the #1 exporter of the gospel
- **Avoid:** Christian nationalism framing, political narrative; stay focused on prophetic calling, not patriotism

### Narrative Direction
- Story is built around knocking the whole time — persistence/perseverance is the through-line
- Climax: at the very end, the door just cracks open slightly — the event itself (M Cup) is the open door
- The opener is a prelude — it cracks the door open for the night, not the full story

### Key Visual Motif — Doors
- Doors as the central visual motif throughout
- Diverse doors from different cultures: yurts in Mongolia, dirt doors on huts in Africa, ornate Mexican/Spanish doors — fast sizzle reel of doors from nations we serve
- People traveling from different places toward a door, overcoming obstacles, arriving to knock
- Knocking = perseverance made visual

### Parable Connection
- The persistent widow (Luke 18) — she knocks until the door opens
- Tie to theme: we persevered in prayer, giving, and sowing — God opened the door

### Nations to Feature
Iran, Cuba, the Maasai (northern Tanzania), Somali refugees in Ethiopia/Kenya. The Messenger X app as a door into closed nations (Saudi Arabia, North Korea).

### Closing Charge
- Lisa's suggested closing line: "Tonight we knock on the doors of the nations"
- Bring the room into the mission together

### Tone and Feel
- Simple and clear — don't overcomplicate the story
- Urgency: the door is open but can shut; "tonight we walk through before it shuts"
- Cinematic and impactful — gut-punch, not abstract
- No live stage elements — she has seen them feel overdone when the concept isn't clear

## Opener Concept

**Runtime:** ~1.5–2.5 minutes
**Plays:** Thursday, July 23 — immediately after dinner countdown, before fundraiser auction
**Emotional goal:** Move the room deeply before paddles go up. Give people goosebumps or make them cry.
**Playback:** Standalone file in ProPresenter, synced with lighting programmer via Ableton

### The Big Idea
A single visual motif — a door — carries the entire story. Scenes of people from different nations press through chaos and opposition to reach the same door. The knocking grows until the door cracks open, revealing the Broadmoor ballroom. The night begins.

### Narrative Arc

**Act 1 — The Door**
A woman stands alone in a dark, abstract space before a massive ancient wooden door. She reaches out and places her palm on it. She doesn't knock yet. She listens.

**Act 2 — The Nations**
Three hero scenes, each from a different nation, showing people pressing through real opposition toward the same door:
- Nigeria: a figure pushes through a crowd, a church burning in the background
- Middle East: a woman moves carefully through a narrow alley, determined
- Southeast Asia: a man rises from a flooded street, exhausted but moving forward

Each scene ends with them arriving at the door. The door looks different in each location — charred, graffitied, ornate — but the same grain of ancient wood runs through all of them.

**Act 3 — Rapid Sequence**
A fast montage (~12–15 shots, ~1 second each) across more nations. The motion stays identical; only the world around them changes. The knocking accelerates and becomes musical, syncing with the score. It becomes almost overwhelming.

**Act 4 — The Crack**
Back to the woman in the dark room. She hears the knocking from the other side. She steps back. A sliver of warm light cuts across the floor — the door is opening, not by her hand.

The camera slowly pulls back. Light floods in. Tables emerge from the darkness. Candles. Paddles on every seat. The Broadmoor ballroom comes into focus.

**Text on screen:**
> *"I have placed before you an open door that no one can shut."* — Revelation 3:8

**Final line:**
> *The nations are knocking. God has opened the door. Tonight, will you answer?*

### Visual Language
- **The door:** Ancient, worn, solid wood — universal. Same door across all nations, slightly different in each environment (char marks, graffiti, clean stone arches) but always the same grain underneath. The door is not the enemy — it is the opportunity.
- **The woman:** Represents the M Cup participants — us. She is on the receiving side. She didn't open it. She witnesses it opening.
- **The nations:** Protagonists pressing through — not running away from the chaos, but through it, toward the door.
- **Light:** Darkness throughout, then a single crack of warm light at the climax.

### Score / Music
- **Primary:** "Kiss the Ring" (found by Josh Kendrick) — slow cinematic build, international instrumentation, strong chime elements, natural stitching points
- **Secondary:** Oppenheimer soundtrack (building track) — may need a mashup for the final crescendo
- **Approach:** The knocking becomes musical as the video progresses, syncing with the score as percussion. Lighting programmer syncs via Ableton to final video.

### Production Approach
- **Option A (AI-assisted):** Use Cling01 to transfer filmed performances (knocking, pressing-through-crowds motion) onto AI-generated characters in different environments. Allows the same emotional performance to read as different people in different nations without filming on location. Consistent character generation via Sora/SolidID (20–80 reference images).
- **Option B (Traditional):** Hire actors representing each nation, film close-up emotion shots in studio (green screen or LED wall), build environments around them. Build a practical door prop.
- **Decision:** Pending tests Andrew is running. Goal is to confirm before Lisa leaves for Italy (~May 6).

**Footage mix:**
- First half: New footage (AI-generated, stock, or newly shot) — cinematic, abstract
- Second half: International field footage mixed with M Cup recap footage — donors giving alongside recipients receiving

### Open Creative Decisions
- Which specific nations to feature in hero shots (Nigeria confirmed; Somalia — get clarity from John)
- Final music selection / mashup approach
- Actors vs. full AI for character work
- Exact structure and order of hero scenes

## Script v2 — Current

*Last revised: 2026-04-29*

[SILENCE — BLACK SCREEN]

A single, massive wooden door. Ancient. Worn.

The room around it is dark. A woman stands before it, close enough to reach. She places her palm flat against the wood.

She feels it. Something is on the other side.

---

[CUT — NIGERIA]

Smoke. Chaos. A crowd pressing outward in panic.

A man moves against the current — into the chaos, not away from it. Shoulder down. Eyes forward.

A church is burning in the background. People scatter.

[CLOSE — HIS FACE]

Unflinching. Not stopping.

He sees it through the smoke: a door. Same worn grain.

He fights toward it. Through the crowd. Through the smoke.

He arrives.

He stands before it.

---

[CUT — MIDDLE EAST]

Narrow streets. Nighttime. A woman moves fast, low against a wall.

Soldiers ahead. She freezes. Waits. They pass.

She turns a corner. Persevering through the danger.

Standing before her is a similar door. Different wall. Same grain.

She checks behind her. Faces it.

She stands before it.

---

[CUT — EUROPE]

A city square. Daytime. A riot has broken out — shouting, pushing, bodies pressing in every direction.

A man is moving through it. Not running from it. Pushing through it.

The crowd shoves back. He staggers. Rights himself.

He perseveres through the bodies, through the noise, through the chaos of a world that has forgotten what it believes.

He sees the door on the other side of the crowd.

He keeps pushing.

He arrives.

He stands before it.

---

[CUT — SOUTHEAST ASIA]

Torrential rain. A flooded street. Water rushing hard.

A woman presses forward — knees against the current, arms out for balance. People around her are retreating. She's not.

Ahead, against the wall stands the door.

She fights through the current. Reaches it.

She stands before it.

---

[MASSIVE LIGHTNING — THUNDER]

A single, shattering bolt.

She doesn't flinch.

---

[MATCH CUT — THE DARK ROOM]

A flash is seen through the door illuminating the woman's face.

She pulls back from the door, startled.

The thunder fades.

---

[RAPID SEQUENCE — ~15 shots, building hard]

The man from Nigeria raises his hand and knocks.

The woman from the Middle East knocks.

Face after face. Hand after hand.

Mongolia. Iran. Cuba. Tanzania. Brazil. United States.

Same wood. Same grain. Same knock.

The knocking multiplies — faster, louder. It becomes music. The rhythm locks in, driving, urgent, building to a wall of sound.

Nation after nation — they have persevered through all of it.

And now they knock.

---

[THE DARK ROOM]

The woman hears knocking build on the opposite side of her door.

She steps back.

The door is opening.

But not by her hand.

Warm light cuts across the floor.

---

[THE DOOR OPENS — LIGHT POURS IN]

The light grows, illuminating the room the woman has been standing in.

Tables emerge from the darkness. Candles flicker to life.

The camera pulls back slowly.

She's been in the Broadmoor the whole time. Paddles rest on every seat.

The room is overcome by the warm light transitioning into b-roll of nations holding resources, intermixing with paddles being raised.

---

[SCRIPTURE ON SCREEN]

> *"Before you, I have placed an open door that no one can shut."*
> — Revelation 3:8

The nations are knocking.

**Tonight — we step through.**
