## [SILENCE — BLACK SCREEN]

A single, massive wooden double door. Ancient. Worn.
The room around it is dark.

A figure stands before it, close enough to reach.
They are drawn to it. They slowly approach the door, curious.

They place their hand against the grain. Searching. Feeling.

**Something is on the other side.**

---

## [CUT — NIGERIA]

Smoke. Chaos. A crowd pressing outward in panic.

A man moves against the current — into the chaos, not away from it. Shoulder down. Eyes forward.
A church is burning in the background. People scatter.

*Unflinching. Not stopping.*

He sees it through the smoke: a door. Same worn grain.

---

## [CUT — IRAN]

Narrow streets. Nighttime. A woman moves fast, low against a wall.
Soldiers ahead, shooting guns in the air. She freezes. Waits. They pass.

She turns a corner, persevering through the danger.

Standing before her is a similar door. Different wall. Same grain.
She checks behind her. Then faces the door.

---

## [CUT — FIRST WORLD NATION]

Rain. Hard, driving rain.

A city street. A crowd has filled it — shouting, signs, the noise of a culture at war with itself.

A man moves through it. Not running from it. Pressing into it.
The crowd shoves back. He staggers. Rights himself. Keeps going.

The rain is relentless. He is soaked, cold, but his eyes stay fixed on the far side of the crowd.

*He sees it.*
A door. Same worn grain.

He pushes through the last of the crowd and arrives.
Refuse and debris have piled against the door — scattered wreckage of the protest, trash, broken things.

A massive peal of thunder and lightning is heard.

---

## [THE DARK ROOM]

The character in the dark room hears a thunderous rattle come from the other side of the door.
A slight flash comes through the crack of the door.

They desperately want to understand what is on the other side.
They begin to feel around for a handle, a latch, something — a way to open the mysterious door.

---

## [CUT — NIGERIA]

The man fights toward his door. Through the crowd. Through the smoke.
A massive burning beam falls in front of him, sending a ball of fire into the air.

---

## [CUT — IRAN]

The door has been sealed deliberately — boards nailed across the frame, a chain looped and knotted at the center.

She starts with the boards. Pries them loose with her bare hands.

---

## [CUT — FIRST WORLD NATION]

The man sees an iron latch.
His hands are shaking. He grabs it.

---

## [MATCH CUT — THE DARK ROOM]

The character beats and pushes against the door, but there is no give.
They step back and fall to their knees before the door, hands pressed flat against it.

---

## [RAPID SEQUENCE — ~12 SHOTS, BUILDING]

The Man in the First World Nation swings open his latch.

The man in Nigeria — the latch breaks free.
The woman in Iran — the latch throws.

---

## [THE DARK ROOM]

The figure kneeling before the door hears nothing.
*Then — something.*

A sound. A shift. Deep inside the door — movement.

A thin line of warm light appears at the base of the door.
Then the edge.

**The door begins to open — not by the figure's hand. By something else entirely.**

---

## [THE DOOR OPENS — LIGHT POURS IN]

The figure lifts their face.

The light is blinding. Warm. Overwhelming.

They rise and reach for the door, pulling it the rest of the way open.

Light floods the room.
