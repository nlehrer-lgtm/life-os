# Messenger International Staff Handbook — Key Reference

*Source: Staff Handbook, Revised June 2023*

---

## 1. Mission, Vision, and Core Values

### Mission
To develop uncompromising followers of Christ who transform our world.

### Vision
Revolutionize global discipleship by providing translated resources to everyone, everywhere — regardless of language, location, or financial situation.

### Background
Founded in 1990 by John & Lisa Bevere. A multi-nation, multi-site 501(c)(3) non-profit religious organization impacting millions through publishing, speaking, leadership development, international broadcast, anti-trafficking/humanitarian work, and MessengerX (discipleship app).

### Core Values (Five Pillars)
1. Purity
2. Power
3. Integrity
4. Excellence
5. Honor

## 2. Team Covenant

- Value every team member and the people we serve
- Trust each other and be people worthy of trust
- Empower team members to succeed in their gifts
- Embrace the thrill and joy of the process
- Challenge and encourage each other and ourselves

- Fight for fierce unity — never allowing gossip, discord, or strife

## 3. Statement of Faith

- **Scripture:** The Bible is God's word — accurate, authoritative, and applicable.
- **God:** One eternal God in three Persons (Father, Son, Holy Spirit) — totally loving and completely holy.
- **Jesus Christ:** Both God and man; lived sinlessly, died on the cross, rose from the dead to give us life.
- **Salvation:** Requires repentance, belief in Jesus Christ, and submission to His will.
- **Holy Spirit:** Transforms lives and empowers with spiritual gifts; baptism in water and filling of the Spirit are important.
- **The Church:** Believers are to meet regularly for fellowship, prayer, and communion.
- **Human Identity:** All human beings are created by God and in His image — including preborn children, elderly, and those marginalized. Gender identity is biologically determined.
- **Sexuality and Marriage:** Scripture depicts marriage as an intimate, sexual relationship exclusively between a man and a woman. Sexual stewardship is a lifelong responsibility.
- **Sin and Healing:** Sin fractures lives, but surrender to God brings healing and transformation.
- **Eternity:** Eternal destination (heaven or hell) is determined by response to Jesus Christ.
- **Second Coming:** Jesus Christ is returning as He promised.

## 4. Employment Policies

### At-Will Employment
Employment is at-will — either party may terminate at any time, with or without cause or notice.

### Introductory Period
First 90 days. Completion does not guarantee continued employment or alter at-will status.

### Employee Categories
| Category | Description |
|---|---|
| **Executive** | Vision casting, resource production, overseeing Directors/Ministry Leads |
| **Directors & Ministry Leads** | Oversee departments; directing, coaching, and leading |
| **Coordinators & Support Services** | Administrative, technical, clerical, or facility care |

### Employee Status
- **Regular Full-Time:** 32+ hours/week; eligible for all benefits
- **Regular Part-Time:** Under 32 hours/week; eligible for some benefits
- **Temporary:** No benefits unless specifically authorized in writing

## 5. Conduct and Behavioral Expectations

### General Conduct
Unacceptable conduct includes: theft, falsification of records, insubordination, sleeping during work hours, illegal drugs/alcohol during work, unauthorized weapons, abusive language, recording time for another employee.

### Dispute Resolution (Matthew 18 Principle)
1. Go directly to the person — in grace and love — just between the two of you
2. If unresolved, bring one or two others who can maintain confidentiality
3. Pray for humility and wisdom; plan your words; choose the right time and place

### Personal Boundaries (Cross-Gender Interactions)
**Meetings:**
- One-on-one meetings should be on-site in areas visible to others
- No closed-door meetings unless room has uncovered windows visible to others
- Off-site meetings: notify direct supervisor and spouse; meet in a public space

**Communication:**
- Conversations should be ministry-related and via work email when possible
- Lengthy or personal conversations should include a 3rd party (spouse or supervisor)

**Travel:**
- Male and female employees generally should not ride alone together
- Overnight travel requires minimum 3 employees, no opposite-sex alone time, separate rooms, Executive Team approval, and spouse notification

**Interns:**
- No romantic relationships or romantically/sexually suggestive behavior between staff and interns, ever

### Corrective Action
May include: coaching, oral/written warnings, performance improvement plans, suspension, demotion, or termination.

## 7. Compensation and Pay

### Pay Periods
- Semi-monthly: paid on the **15th** and the **last day** of each month
- If payday falls on a weekend or holiday, paid on the preceding workday
- Workweek begins at 12:00 a.m. Sunday

### Timekeeping (Non-Exempt)
- Clock in no more than 5 minutes early; clock out no more than 5 minutes late
- Must clock in/out for lunch periods; lunch periods are unpaid

### Overtime
- Non-exempt employees: 1.5x regular rate for hours over 40 in a workweek
- Must be authorized in advance by a supervisor/manager

### Expense Reimbursement
- Requires pre-approval from direct supervisor, submission of receipts, and uploading to the appropriate app
- Ministry credit cards must be used for ministry purchases whenever possible

### Advances and Loans
MI does not give advances or loans to employees.

## 8. Benefits

### Paid Holidays (Full-Time)
- New Year's Day
- Good Friday (half day)
- Memorial Day
- Independence Day
- Labor Day
- Thanksgiving + Friday after Thanksgiving
- Christmas Eve + Christmas Day
- New Year's Eve (half day)

### Sick Leave
| Employee Type | Sick Leave |
|---|---|
| **Full-Time** | 48 hours granted as lump sum at start of each benefit year |
| **Part-Time** | 1 hour per 30 hours worked, up to 48 hours/year |
| **Rollover Cap** | 72 hours maximum |

Sick leave may be used for: illness/injury/preventive care (self or family), public health emergencies, domestic violence situations, bereavement, and mental health days ("Well Days," with prior manager approval). Not paid out at separation.

### Paid Time Off (PTO)
| Years of Employment | Days | Hours | Rollover Cap |
|---|---|---|---|
| Years 0–1 | 7 days | 56 hours | 80 hours |
| Years 2–4 | 12 days | 96 hours | 120 hours |
| Years 5+ | 17 days | 136 hours | 160 hours |

- PTO begins accruing immediately; usable after introductory period
- Unused PTO is **paid out upon separation**
- **PTO Blackout:** Not approved during Messenger Cup (including 3 weeks prior and 1 week after)

### PTO Donation
Employees can donate unused PTO to a colleague with a medical emergency. Both parties must have 1+ year of employment.

### Health Insurance
- For employees working 30+ hours/week
- Coverage begins the 1st of the month following hire/eligibility

### Retirement (403(b))
- Begins after **6 months** of continuous employment
- MI contributes **3% of eligible employee's gross annual salary**
- Employees may also contribute via payroll deduction

### Cell Phone Allowance
Available for roles that require regular cell phone use; requires supervisor and HR Director approval.

## 9. Leave Policies

### Pregnancy Leave
- Requires **3 full years of service** and full-time status
- Up to **3 consecutive weeks** of paid leave at regular rate of pay
- Runs concurrently with FMLA; in addition to PTO and sick leave
- Notice required no later than the 20th week before expected arrival

### Parental Leave
- Requires **3 full years of service** and full-time status
- Up to **1 week** of paid parental leave for birth or adoption
- Must be taken within the first 12 months following birth or placement

### Bereavement Leave
- Up to **3 paid working days** upon death of an immediate family member
- Immediate family: parent, spouse, child, sibling, grandchild, parent-in-law, grandparent, step-relatives, anyone in same household

### Personal Leave of Absence
- Available after 6+ months; unpaid
- Requires written request; approval at MI's sole discretion
- Benefits do not accrue during unpaid leave
- Failure to return on scheduled date = assumed resignation

### Jury Service Leave
- Paid for up to 3 days; use accrued PTO for longer service

### Voting Leave
- Up to 3 paid hours if needed

## 10. Work Hours and Schedule

### Standard Hours
- Monday–Friday, 8:00 a.m.–5:00 p.m. local time
- **Required in-office days: Monday, Tuesday, and Thursday**
- Wednesday and Friday are remote-eligible by default

### Daily Schedule Structure
- **Team Devotions:** Every workday, 8:00–8:20 a.m. Mountain Time
  - Mon/Tue/Thu: in person (or Zoom for those without a worksite)
  - Wed/Fri: via Zoom
  - Cameras on at least 3 days/week
- **Focus Time (no interdepartmental meetings):**
  - Mon–Thu: 9:00 a.m.–12:00 p.m. Mountain Time
  - Friday: 1:00–5:00 p.m. Mountain Time
- **Meetings:** Should wrap up by 4:00 p.m. Mountain / 5:00 p.m. Central

### Meal and Rest Periods
- 15-minute rest break for every 4 hours worked
- 60-minute unpaid meal period for shifts of 5+ hours

### Remote Work
- WFH is a privilege, not a right; can be revoked at any time
- Recurring/extended WFH arrangements considered after 12 months of employment

## 11. Health, Safety, and Security

### Drug and Alcohol Policy
- **Marijuana:** Employees asked to refrain except in rare medical circumstances
- **Alcohol:** Must abstain at all Ministry events, in public settings representing MI, and around recovering individuals; drunkenness is never acceptable
- **Illegal drugs:** Strictly prohibited during MI-related duties
- MI reserves the right to drug/alcohol test; refusal = treated as positive test

### Non-Smoking
No smoking in any MI buildings, facilities, worksites, or vehicles. Must be 20+ feet from entrances/exits/windows.

### Workplace Violence
Zero tolerance. Any threat or violent act is grounds for immediate termination.

## 12. Workplace Guidelines

### Attendance
- Notify manager at least 30 minutes before start of scheduled workday if absent or late
- 3 consecutive unnotified absences = assumed voluntary resignation

### Personal Appearance
Work-appropriate attire required. Not acceptable: flip flops, spandex/workout attire, sagging clothing, sexually provocative clothing, offensive slogans.

### Confidentiality
Do not disclose internal reports, financials, donor lists, trade secrets, or other confidential information without authorization.

### Social Media
Personal accounts must reflect Christ and the ministry — respectful, honest, accurate, Christ-honoring. No confidential MI information; no speaking as an official MI spokesperson without authorization.

### Intellectual Property
All work created in the scope of employment belongs to MI.

### Technology Use
All MI-issued hardware, software, and internet connections are MI property. MI may monitor all communications, email, files, and internet usage at any time.

## 13. Employment Separation

### Resignation
- At least 2 weeks written notice requested
- Directors/Executives should give as much notice as practical
- MI may ask an employee not to work their notice period

### Return of Property
All MI property must be returned immediately at termination.
