## Fit Assessment

**Score: 9/10**

This is a near-perfect fit. Blue-collar specialty shop in Franklin — the exact market. Their trucks are visually compelling and built for social media, yet their presence is anemic (under 250 Instagram followers after 97 posts). No professional video, no owner face, no story. The builds do the talking — they just need someone to film it right.

Worth noting: Moody's Tire & Auto Service has been prospected separately. The Lift Shop has its own brand, its own Instagram, its own website, and a distinctly different customer — truck enthusiasts and off-road guys, not just tire-and-oil customers. These are two separate pitches, but having a warm relationship with the Moody family through the tire shop side is a significant door-opener here.

## Cold Call Prep

**Local connection angle:**
You're a Spring Hill guy — you probably drive past or near their Columbia Ave shop regularly. The Moody name is woven into Franklin history. If you've already made contact on the Moody's Tire side, use that directly: "I spoke with your team about the tire shop — I wanted to circle back specifically about the Lift Shop because I think there's a real opportunity there."

**What to genuinely compliment:**
Their builds are clean. The work speaks for itself — lifted trucks, custom wheels, leveling kits that actually look intentional. Whatever they're doing mechanically, it's clearly quality. That's the hook: the product is great, the content just doesn't show it.

**Suggested opener:**
"Hey, this is Nathaniel — I'm a local videographer based in Spring Hill, and I work with a handful of businesses in the Franklin area doing monthly content. I've been following the Lift Shop's Instagram and honestly, the trucks you guys are building are incredible — I just think the content you're getting doesn't do the work justice. I'd love to come film for an hour and show you what I mean, totally free the first month."

**Levi's 4 questions — pre-answered:**
1. How much of my time will this take? — "About 10–15 minutes — that's it."
2. What am I actually going to get? — "A full month of polished photo and video content — truck reveal clips, build shots, the kind of stuff that makes people want to book."
3. How long until I get it? — "You'll have your first batch of content within a week of the shoot."
4. What will this do for me? — "It builds trust before a customer ever picks up the phone to call you. Guys shopping for a $5,000 lift kit are doing their homework on Instagram first."

**Likely objection:**
"We already post on Instagram and Facebook pretty regularly."

**How to handle it:**
"That's great — and honestly the builds you're putting out are solid. What I do is just add a layer on top of that. Same builds, same shop — just filmed with real gear and edited so it actually stops the scroll. You keep doing what you're doing; I just make sure more people see it."
