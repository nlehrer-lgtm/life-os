# Jobsite Social — Brand Guide

**Version 1.0** | Spring Hill, Tennessee | Owner: Nathaniel Lehrer

---

## 1. Brand at a Glance

**Jobsite Social** is a local social media content service for blue-collar and service-based small businesses in Middle Tennessee. One shoot a month. A full month of polished short-form video. Owners stay in their boots — not behind a camera.

We are not an agency. We are a neighbor with a camera who knows how to make your work look as good as it actually is.

> **Mission:** Make trust-building easy for the people who build, fix, and serve our community.

> **One-line pitch:** *"People don't just check Google anymore — they want to see who they're working with before they call."*

---

## 2. Brand DNA

### Brand Pillars

1. **Built Like the Trades** — Bold, no-nonsense, dependable. We show up when we say we will and we deliver.
2. **Neighbor First** — Local. Familiar. Community over commerce. We work with our neighbors, not at them.
3. **Easy on the Owner** — One hour on-site. 10–15 minutes of their time. We stay out of the way.
4. **Polished, Not Polished-Up** — High craft. Real people. Never fake, scripted, or corporate.

### Who We Serve

- Single-location, owner-operator service businesses
- $750K–$3M annual revenue
- Trades where social media isn't yet table stakes (auto, HVAC, plumbing, roofing, electrical, pressure washing, detailing, flooring, landscaping — and adjacent categories)
- Located within Spring Hill / Thompson's Station / Franklin / Brentwood / Columbia

### Who We Are NOT

- ❌ A corporate marketing agency
- ❌ Boutique, artisan, or "creative-led"
- ❌ Stock graphics, AI slop, or canned templates
- ❌ A national/scaled SaaS platform
- ❌ Salesy, pushy, or jargon-heavy

## 3. Voice & Tone

### Voice Principles

| Principle | What It Means | What To Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| **Confident, not salesy** | Speak plainly. Own the value. | "Synergy," "leverage," "ROI-driven solutions" |
| **Helpful, not pushy** | Lead with what's in it for them. | High-pressure asks, fake urgency |
| **Clear, not complex** | A 9th grader should understand every word. | Jargon, acronyms, agency-speak |
| **Warm, not corporate** | Talk like a neighbor. Use first names. | Stiff, formal, third-person company-speak |
| **Specific, not vague** | Name the trade, name the town, name the truck. | "Various industries," "in your area" |

### Voice in Action

✅ **Yes:**
- *"I come out for about an hour, stay out of your way, and you've got a full month of content."*
- *"You guys do great work — we'd love to help more people see it."*
- *"No contract. No catch. Free month, then you decide."*

❌ **No:**
- *"Our team leverages best-in-class production workflows to deliver omnichannel content solutions."*
- *"We're disrupting the local marketing space with a SaaS-enabled content engine."*

### Words We Use

- Shop, jobsite, crew, owner, neighbor, real, simple, quick, polished, trust, show up
- Spring Hill, Franklin, Middle Tennessee, local, around here, down the road

### Words We Don't

- Synergy, leverage, omnichannel, ROI, deliverables, ecosystem, scalable, disrupt, optimize, growth hack, funnel, KPI

## 4. Logo System

### Primary Lockup — Horizontal
The horizontal lockup is the primary mark. Use it whenever space allows.

- **"JOBSITE"** — Asphalt Black
- **"SOCIAL"** — Jobsite Yellow
- **Top-left bracket** — Jobsite Yellow (opens the frame)
- **Bottom-right bracket** — Asphalt Black (closes the frame)

The brackets reference a camera viewfinder — a quiet nod to the work we do.

**File:** `logos/jobsite-social-horizontal-color.svg`

### Stacked Lockup
Use when horizontal space is constrained (square IG profile crops, narrow ads, vertical signage).

**File:** `logos/jobsite-social-stacked-color.svg`

### Icon / Monogram
Use as a profile picture, favicon, or watermark when the full lockup won't read. Two camera brackets framing **"JS"**.

**File:** `logos/jobsite-social-icon-color.svg`

### Wordmark Only
For applications where the brackets compete with surrounding design (e.g., embedded in dense layouts or stamped on photography).

**File:** `logos/jobsite-social-wordmark-color.svg`

### Monochrome Variants

| Variant | When To Use |
|---|---|
| **All-Black** | Single-color print, embroidery, light backgrounds |
| **All-White** (knockout) | Dark backgrounds, photo overlays, video lower-thirds |

### Clear Space

Minimum clear space around the logo = the height of the "J" in JOBSITE on all sides. Never let other elements crowd the mark.

### Minimum Sizes

- **Horizontal:** 120px wide (digital) / 1 inch wide (print)
- **Stacked:** 100px wide (digital) / 0.75 inch wide (print)
- **Icon:** 32px (favicon) / 0.5 inch (print)

### Logo Don'ts

- ❌ Don't recolor the wordmark outside the approved palette
- ❌ Don't stretch, skew, or rotate the lockup
- ❌ Don't add drop shadows, gradients, or outlines
- ❌ Don't place the color version on a yellow or near-yellow background
- ❌ Don't break the brackets apart from the wordmark in lockup variants
- ❌ Don't reorder the words ("Social Jobsite" — never)

| **Asphalt Black** | "JOBSITE," bottom-right bracket, body text | `#1A1A1A` | 26 / 26 / 26 | 0 / 0 / 0 / 90 | PMS Black 6 C |
| **Concrete White** | Backgrounds, knockout variants | `#FFFFFF` | 255 / 255 / 255 | 0 / 0 / 0 / 0 | — |

### Secondary

| Name | Use | HEX | RGB |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Steel Gray** | Secondary text, dividers, subtle UI | `#4A4A4A` | 74 / 74 / 74 |
| **Galvanized** | Borders, table rules, soft surfaces | `#D9D9D9` | 217 / 217 / 217 |

### Accent (Use Sparingly)

| Name | Use | HEX | RGB |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Caution Orange** | High-emphasis CTAs, alert moments | `#F26522` | 242 / 101 / 34 |

### Color Rules

- Yellow is the heart of the brand. Use it deliberately — not everywhere. It works because of what surrounds it.
- Black + white + yellow is always the default combination. Add Steel Gray when you need a third neutral.
- Never sit Jobsite Yellow on Caution Orange. Never sit Jobsite Yellow on Galvanized (low contrast).
- All color combinations used for text must meet WCAG AA contrast (4.5:1 for body text).

## 6. Typography

### Display / Headlines — Bold Condensed

The wordmark and headlines should feel like stenciled equipment lettering — tall, condensed, all-caps.

**Primary (Free / Google Fonts):** **Anton** — single weight, condensed, free, easy to deploy on web.
**Alternate (Free / Google Fonts):** **Bebas Neue** — slightly more refined, great for body display.
**Premium upgrade:** Industry Inc, Druk Wide, or Forza if a paid license fits later.

Use Anton or Bebas Neue at **ALL CAPS** for headlines, section titles, and short callouts.

### Body / UI — Condensed Sans

**Primary (Free / Google Fonts):** **Barlow Condensed** — pairs perfectly with Anton, reads cleanly at small sizes, has the work-wear feel.

Use sentence case. Body weight 400 (regular) or 500 (medium). Headlines 700 (bold).

### Web/Email Fallback Stack

```css
font-family: "Anton", "Oswald", "Arial Black", sans-serif;       /* display */
font-family: "Barlow Condensed", "Roboto Condensed", Arial, sans-serif;  /* body */
```

### Typographic Rules

- Display = ALL CAPS, no exceptions
- Body = sentence case (we are not yelling at our clients)
- Line spacing on body: 1.4–1.5x
- Letter spacing on display: +25 to +50 (subtle tracking to feel structural)
- Italics: avoid. We are not a magazine.

## 7. Photography & Video Style

This is the heart of the deliverable. Style here directly mirrors what we shoot for clients.

### Look & Feel

- **Real, not staged.** Owners in their actual environment. Crews mid-job. Tools mid-use.
- **Strong natural light** wherever possible. Cinematic but never overproduced.
- **Warm contrast.** Slight teal/orange grade. Black point lifted slightly to keep a documentary feel.
- **Faces, hands, equipment, materials.** In that order of priority.

### Composition

- 3:2 or 4:5 (photo) — vertical 9:16 for Reels/Shorts
- Tight on the action: hands on the wrench, brush on the wall, boot on the gas pedal
- Wide environment shots that establish the shop or jobsite
- Owner-on-camera: chest-up, jobsite or shop visible behind them, soft natural light

### What We Don't Shoot

- ❌ Stock-feeling smiley headshots against gray seamless
- ❌ Drone shots that don't serve the story
- ❌ Anything that looks like a corporate explainer video
- ❌ Group shots posed like a high-school yearbook

### Edit Pace

- Cuts feel rhythmic, not frantic
- Owner sound bites lead — visuals serve the words
- Lower thirds: Anton, white on Jobsite Yellow swatch, lower-left
- Captions on every Reel (95% of viewers watch with sound off)
