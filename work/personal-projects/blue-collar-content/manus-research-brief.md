# Manus Research Brief — Blue-Collar Content Business: New Category Prospects

**Prepared for:** Nathaniel Lehrer
**Date:** May 2026
**Task:** Wide prospect research across seven new service-business categories in Middle Tennessee.

---

## Context

Nathaniel runs a local social media content service for service businesses in Middle Tennessee, modeled on **autoshop.social**. The offering is simple:

- One on-site shoot per month, roughly one hour
- 10–15 minutes of the owner's time for talking clips
- Delivered as a full month of polished short-form video content (photos pulled as byproducts)
- Pricing target: **$1,500/month per client**

He has already built a 41-business prospect list in trades adjacent categories (auto repair, tire, body shops, HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roofing, pressure washing, detailing, tint/wrap/PPF, epoxy floors, flooring). This brief asks you to research **seven NEW categories** he has not yet covered.

---

## The Strategic Filter (Most Important)

Target categories where **social media is not yet considered normal or expected**. When polished video content shows up in those categories, it becomes a *trust differentiator* — not table stakes. Avoid creative-leaning, merch-driven, or already-social-saturated categories.

---

## Hard Targeting Criteria

A great prospect meets ALL of these:

1. **Single brick-and-mortar location** — work happens at the shop, not primarily at customer sites
2. **Owner-operator** — owner is on-site most of the time and controls the marketing budget personally
3. **Predictable weekly schedule** — same hours, same routine
4. **Year-round operation** — not seasonal-only
5. **Estimated $1M+ annual revenue** — soft floor of $750K acceptable; $1M is the target
6. **Marketing budget culture** — industry typically pays for outside marketing services in the $1,000–$2,500/month range
7. **Weak, stale, or absent social media** — dead Facebook pages, no presence, or thin/private profiles. NOT acceptable: businesses with strong, active, engaging social media already.

---

## Categories to Research (Seven Tier 1 Categories)

For each category, find as many prospects as possible (target: 8–15 per category) in the geography below.

### 1. Multi-agent independent insurance agencies
- Erie, Farm Bureau, multi-carrier brokerages, Allstate agencies with multiple agents under one owner.
- **Exclude:** solo captive State Farm or Allstate agents (corporate controls their budget).

### 2. Outdoor power equipment dealers
- Independent Stihl, Husqvarna, Toro, Bad Boy, Cub Cadet, Echo dealers with retail floor + service department.
- **Exclude:** chain hardware stores (Lowe's, Home Depot, Tractor Supply).

### 3. Independent hearing aid / audiology clinics
- Locally-owned. **Exclude:** Miracle-Ear, Beltone, HearUSA, or any corporate franchise location.

### 4. Independent veterinary clinics
- Small to mid-size (1–4 vets), locally-owned. **Exclude:** corporate chains (VCA, Banfield, BluePearl, Thrive).

### 5. Full-service brick-and-mortar car washes
- Independent, full-service tunnel washes or hand washes with detailing options.
- **Exclude:** touchless gas-station washes, pure self-serve bays.

### 6. Auto glass shops
- With commercial/fleet/insurance volume, not just mobile-only chip repair operators. Independent, locally-owned.
- **Exclude:** Safelite (corporate chain).

### 7. Gun shops with retail + range or classes
- Independent FFLs with retail floor AND either an indoor range, training/class offerings, or both.
- **Exclude:** pure online FFLs without storefront, pawn-shop hybrids.

## Scoring Rubric (1–10)

- **10** — Perfect fit: single location, $1M+ revenue signal, weak/dead/absent social, owner-operated, marketing-budget-friendly category
- **9** — Strong fit with minor caveat (e.g., revenue uncertain but signals strong, OR social presence present but very stale)
- **8** — Good fit with one notable weakness (revenue likely under $1M but in a target category, OR thin social presence)
- **7** — Worth a call but lower priority (active-but-generic social media, or below revenue target)
- **6 or lower** — Low priority: actively engaging social media already, multi-location chain, or corporate franchise

## Categories to Explicitly Exclude

Do NOT include businesses from these categories — they are either creative/merch-driven, already social-saturated, or off-model on revenue or budget:

- Custom builders, woodworkers, makers, refinishers
- Tattoo and piercing shops
- Boutique fitness studios (yoga, Pilates, CrossFit, spin, barre)
- Coffee shops, bakeries, florists, restaurants, bars
- Salons, hair stylists, nail salons, men's grooming boutiques, barbershops
- Photographers and videographers
- Custom car, hot rod, or classic car restoration shops
- Med spas, aesthetic clinics
- Anything self-described as "boutique," "artisan," "handcrafted," or "custom"
- Solo captive State Farm or Allstate insurance agents
- Independent pharmacies (margins too tight to support marketing spend)
- Solo CPAs and small tax preparers
- Driving schools, small engine repair shops, bait & tackle, feed stores, auto upholstery — revenue typically below threshold
