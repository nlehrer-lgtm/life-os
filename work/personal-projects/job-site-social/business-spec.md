# [[Job Site Social]] — Business Spec & Explainer

*A local social media content service for the blue-collar shops, service businesses, and trade professionals who build the towns we live in. Built and operated by Nathaniel Lehrer in Spring Hill, Tennessee.*

---

## The Quick Pitch

Most great local businesses are nearly invisible online. The work is excellent, the reputation is real, but when a new customer Googles them — or worse, searches Instagram or Facebook — there's nothing there.

Job Site Social fixes that. We show up once a month, spend about an hour on-site, and produce a full month of polished, personality-driven social media content the owner doesn't have to think about. No long contracts. No production crews. No disruption to the workday.

The result: when a customer goes looking for who they're about to hire, they find a real person, a real team, and real work — and trust is built before the phone ever rings.

---

## The Problem We Solve

There's a quiet shift happening in how customers decide who to trust. Google reviews still matter, but more and more, people are pulling up a business's Instagram or Facebook before they call. They want to see a face. They want to see the team. They want to see the work.

For most blue-collar and service businesses, that audit doesn't go well:

- Dead Facebook pages with the last post from 2019
- A few static graphics with the logo and a quote
- The occasional vertical phone clip with bad audio and no edit
- Or — most commonly — nothing at all

These businesses aren't bad at their craft. They're great at it. They just don't have the time, the skill, or the equipment to translate that quality into something a stranger can see in 15 seconds on their phone.

That's the gap. And in a market like Middle Tennessee, where the population is growing faster than the local-business reputation can travel, it's costing them new customers every week.

---

## What We Do

**One on-site shoot per month. About one hour. Ten to fifteen minutes of the owner's time. A full month of content, ready to post.**

Specifically, every month a client gets:

- **Professional video content** — short-form vertical clips edited for Instagram, Facebook, and TikTok. Real footage of the team working, the owner talking, the craft on display.
- **Professional photo content** — pulled as a natural byproduct of the shoot. Suitable for the website, Google Business profile, and static social posts.
- **Optional posting and scheduling** — content gets queued and posted across the agreed platforms throughout the month, so the owner doesn't have to think about it.

The owner's involvement is small and predictable: 10–15 minutes for some quick talking clips (who they are, what they do, why they do it). The rest of the hour is spent quietly capturing the team in their normal workday.

---

## How It Works (The Client Experience)

1. **Free first shoot.** No contract, no commitment, no cost. We show up, capture content, edit and deliver. The owner gets to see exactly what a month of polished content looks like before spending a dollar.
2. **Continue or walk away.** If the work moves the needle, the owner continues at the agreed monthly rate. If it doesn't, no obligation.
3. **Monthly cadence.** One shoot per month. One hour on-site. A new month of content every 30 days.
4. **Owned by the client.** All content produced is the exclusive property of the business. They keep it forever, whether they continue the engagement or not.

## Pricing

- **First month: free.** Always. No exceptions, no contract, no catch.
- **Introductory rate:** starting at $750/month for the right local partners — single-location, owner-operated shops in Williamson and Maury County.
- **Standard rate:** $1,500/month — framed against the cost of a part-time social media manager (typically $2,500–$4,000/month). For roughly half the price, a business gets professional, on-site, original content instead of generic templated posts.
- **No long-term contracts.** Month-to-month. Continue when the work is producing results. Stop when it isn't.

Content ownership stays with the client at every tier.

## Who This Is For (Ideal Client Profile)

The strongest fits share these traits:

- **Single-location, owner-operated.** The owner is on-site most days and controls the marketing budget.
- **Blue-collar or skilled trade.** Auto repair, HVAC, electrical, plumbing, roofing, pressure washing, lawn care, detailing, epoxy, flooring, and similar.
- **Established reputation.** Strong reviews, real word-of-mouth, years in business. They've already earned the trust — they just aren't showing it.
- **Weak or absent social media.** No Instagram. A Facebook that died in 2019. A page with 20 followers and stock photos. The bigger the gap between *what they actually do* and *what's visible online*, the more dramatic the result.
- **Year-round operation.** Predictable schedule, predictable team, content that compounds month over month.
- **A category where social media isn't yet expected.** When polished video shows up in a category that hasn't seen it yet, the business doesn't just catch up — it leapfrogs everyone around it.

The geography is Spring Hill, Thompson's Station, Franklin, Brentwood, and Columbia. The neighborhood is the point. We live where our clients live.

## What Makes This Different

**vs. doing it themselves.** Phone clips, no audio, posted whenever there's time — every business owner knows this isn't working, and most of them feel guilty about it. We remove the guilt and the workload in one move.

**vs. static graphics from a generic agency.** A logo and a quote on a colored background doesn't build trust. A real face, a real shop, and real work does. Our content shows *people*, not templates.

**vs. a full social media agency.** Most agencies charge $2,500–$5,000/month, manage strategy on a Zoom call, and post stock-feeling content the business owner doesn't recognize. We show up in person, capture the actual business, and deliver content the owner is proud of — for less.

**vs. influencer-style marketing.** This isn't about going viral. It's about being the obvious choice when a neighbor recommends a shop and someone Googles them. Trust at the moment of decision. That's the whole win.
