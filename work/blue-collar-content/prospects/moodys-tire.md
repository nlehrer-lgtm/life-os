## Cold Call Prep

**Local connection angle:**
Moody's Franklin location is on Columbia Ave — you drive that road constantly. Their service area explicitly includes Spring Hill. Their shop has been a Franklin institution since 1944. You can reference passing the shop or knowing people in Franklin who've used them — this is genuinely your neighborhood.

**What to genuinely compliment:**
82 years in Franklin. Three generations. That's remarkable staying power — it means they're doing something deeply right with their customers. Their Google reviews back it up (4.6 stars, 500+ reviews). Compliment their longevity and reputation: "You guys have been a fixture in Franklin forever — that kind of trust is rare."

**Suggested opener:**
"Hey Jimmy, it's Nathaniel — I'll keep this real quick. I drive by your shop on Columbia Ave all the time, and I've been following you guys on social media — love seeing a real Franklin institution still going strong. I wanted to reach out because I think there's something awesome that could be done with your story. What would you think about me coming in and getting you a full month of content — completely free?"

**Levi's 4 questions — pre-answered for this prospect:**
1. How much of my time will this take? — "About 10–15 minutes — that's it."
2. What am I actually going to get? — "A full month of polished photo and video content, ready to post."
3. How long until I get it? — [Fill in your standard turnaround]
4. What will this do for me? — "It builds trust before a customer ever picks up the phone to call you — and with 82 years of story to tell, you've got more to work with than almost anybody."

**Likely objection:**
"We've been doing fine without it for 82 years." (Or a variation: we stay busy through word-of-mouth / we're not really a social media business.) This is the most likely pushback — a legacy shop that doesn't feel urgency to change.

**How to handle it:**
"Totally — and honestly, that reputation is exactly why this works so well for you. You've already built the trust. This just puts it where people are looking now. You've got three generations of story that nobody's ever seen on video — I just want to show you what that could look like. And it costs you nothing to find out."
