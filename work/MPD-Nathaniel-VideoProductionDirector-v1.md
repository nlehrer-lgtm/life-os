## 1. Primary Aim of the Role

> *Why does this role exist? What does the organization lose if it disappears?*

This role exists to galvanize the video production team and ensure every team member has the tools, knowledge, and direction needed to do their job at the highest level — while personally owning the final quality standard and systems architecture that allow the team to operate at peak efficiency.

The Director of Video Production is the connective tissue between the ministry's creative vision and what actually gets produced. Without it:

- **[[Andrew McIntosh]] and [[Lindy Wood]]** would have to absorb leadership and direction themselves, forcing them into roles they haven't been developed for yet — leading to blind spots and dropped balls.
- **Quality would slip** in areas where I still step in to correct color grades, lighting plots, script decisions, and alignment with the ministry's mission and clarity standard.
- **Team galvanization would collapse.** No one else on the team has Galvanizing as a Working Genius. The intentional celebration, vision-casting, and rallying around the mission would go away — and the team would feel it.
- **Systems would stagnate.** Nobody is actively designing and iterating on workflows, processes, and gear decisions the way this role does.

## 2. Strategic Objective

> *What does "doing this role well" look like in measurable terms?*

### Key Metrics I Own

1. **On-time, excellent delivery** — All video needs (asked of or required of the team) are delivered on time and to our quality standard.
2. **Team health** — No burnout, no misalignment of roles, no sustained low morale. Everyone is doing the right work.
3. **Innovation and growth** — We are not settling for old systems, old tech, or old ways. We are constantly iterating on processes, gear, and skills.

### Quality Standard for Key Outputs

- **Technical quality** (color grading, audio, lighting, pacing, editing): Buck stops with me.
- **Clarity and impact** (storytelling, mission alignment, audience effect): Judged by [[Lisa Bevere]], [[John Bevere]], [[Addison Bevere]], and the executive team.
- The split is clear: I own *how* it looks and sounds; they own *what it says and whether it lands*.

### Current Targets (Next 6–12 Months)

1. **Podcast system refinement** — Make our podcast capture, prep, and editing pipeline a well-oiled machine. Currently clunky; needs to be systematized.
2. **Studio excellence** — Continue refining our LED wall studio: lighting, camera technique, virtual production quality.
3. **DaVinci Resolve mastery** — Team-wide improvement in color grading and editing efficiency in Resolve.
4. **Creative outlet** — Begin carving space for short-form creative content and short films that go beyond recurring deliverables — a team health and mission goal combined.

## 4. Management Strategy (Operating Rhythm)

### Weekly Meeting Cadence

**Daily (Mon, Tue, Thu, Fri) — Morning Debrief (~15 min)**
- Quick team huddle or Zoom call
- Each person (including me) shares their "Big Three" — top three priorities for the day
- [[Lindy Wood]] leads and kicks it off
- No debrief on Wednesdays

**Every Tuesday — [[Get REC'D]] Weekly Meeting**
- Most important team meeting of the week
- REC'D = Resilience, Excellence, Communication, Discipline (our team pillars)
- [[Lindy Wood]] leads coordination and scheduling rundown
- I share updates, call-outs (wins + growth areas), and mission/vision reinforcement
- Full week agenda reviewed and aligned

**Bi-weekly — 1:1 with [[Lindy Wood]]**
- Lindy leads (we're moving toward subordinate-led 1:1s)
- Feedback in both directions, personal check-in, goal/milestone review
- I act as coach rather than manager

**Bi-weekly — 1:1 with [[Andrew McIntosh]]**
- Same format as Lindy's 1:1
- Emphasis on growth, feedback, and tackling any communication or proactivity friction

**Bi-weekly — 1:1 with [[Arden Bevere]] (upward)**
- I'm the subordinate in this one; Arden coaches me
- Surface anything I need clarity or guidance on

**Bi-monthly — Leadership Team Meeting**
- Ministry-wide leadership development session
- I'm a participant; investment in me as a leader

### How Work Gets Assigned

1. Tasks surface in the **[[Get REC'D]] weekly meeting** and daily debriefs
2. Assigned to individuals based on skill, capacity, and current load — I make final calls on who owns what
3. All non-trivial tasks are logged and tracked in **Asana**
4. Lindy manages coordination and scheduling logistics underneath that

### Feedback Flow

- I give feedback in 1:1s, in team call-outs during [[Get REC'D]], and directly via Frame.io for video reviews
- Frame.io comments are detailed — questions, specific fixes, clarity checks
- I ask personal questions in 1:1s to gauge health before leading with performance
- We are shifting to subordinate-led 1:1s so they surface their own questions and growth needs

### Handling Underperformance or Missed Deadlines

- With a small enough team, I can usually see a deadline approaching and intervene before the miss with coaching
- When something is missed, I follow up directly: what happened, what we learn, make sure it doesn't repeat
- For quality misses: nothing gets approved until it hits the standard. If something slips through, a direct conversation happens about what was missed and why

- **When the team needs to be rallied or re-energized** → That's on me. Andrew and Lindy are not Galvanizers. I use the [[Get REC'D]] weekly to set vision, celebrate wins, and reconnect the team to the mission.

## 9. Risks, Gaps, and Open Questions

### Personal Dependencies (Bus Factor Risks)

- **[[Andrew McIntosh]] and Unreal Engine / LED wall:** If Andrew can't be there, the team can follow SOPs but cannot improvise. No one else truly knows Unreal Engine. This is the highest single-point-of-failure on the team.
- **My quality oversight:** I still personally catch and correct things that would otherwise ship at a lower quality standard. Lindy is being developed to absorb more QC responsibility, but the full handoff hasn't happened.
- **My galvanization:** No one else on the team has Galvanizing as a genius. If I'm not present and investing, team morale and mission-alignment suffers.

### Knowledge at Risk of Being Lost

- LED wall/Unreal Engine operational knowledge (almost entirely in Andrew's head)
- My judgment calls on storytelling quality and ministry alignment
- Workflow decisions and priorities that live in my head and Slack, not in any document

### Fragile Systems or Relationships

- **No one owns infrastructure and tech architecture.** We work with extremely high-end gear but don't have an engineer. When something breaks or needs reconfiguring, the team troubleshoots by instinct and ChatGPT — which works until it doesn't.
- **Shot lists, briefs, and SOPs are stored in Slack** — not organized, not findable, not transferable to a new team member.
- **Physical workspace cleanliness and professional set organization** — a persistent gap. The team doesn't naturally maintain a professional film environment. I coach it constantly but it doesn't stick.
- **The 2030 vision vs. current team capacity:** We are building toward 30 podcasts/30 books by 2030. At current team size (5 people, one studio), taking on 20+ shows domestically is a calendar and capacity impossibility. Nobody at the leadership level is seriously modeling what infrastructure — studio size, team headcount — would actually need to exist to execute the vision. The studio will need to be significantly larger.

### Things I Know I Should Be Doing But Aren't

- Building a real SOP library — written, organized, accessible
- Better gear documentation and tech architecture mapping
- More intentional development of Josh toward professionality and reliability
- Creative outlet work (short films, proactive content) — we want it but it keeps getting pushed by recurring deliverables
