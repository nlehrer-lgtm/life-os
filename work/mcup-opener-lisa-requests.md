# MCUP Opener — Lisa's Requested Elements
*From the April 8, 2026 "MCUP Opener Discussion" meeting*

---

## Theme & Scripture

- **Scripture anchor**: Revelation 3:8 — *"I have placed before you an open door that no one can shut. I know that you have little strength, yet you have kept my word and not denied my name."* (Church of Philadelphia)
- **Central idea**: America's *prophetic mandate* — not her greatness as a nation, but her role as the #1 exporter of the gospel
- **Avoid**: Christian nationalism framing, political narrative around Trump; stay focused on prophetic calling, not patriotism

---

## Narrative Arc (Lisa's Preferred Direction)

- The story is built around **knocking the whole time** — persistence/perseverance is the through-line
- **Climax**: at the very end, the door just **cracks open slightly** — the event itself (MCUP) *is* the open door
- The opener is a **prelude** — it cracks the door open for the night, not the full story of everything happening that night

---

## Key Visual Motif — Doors

- Doors as the **central visual motif** throughout
- **Diverse doors from different cultures**: yurts in Mongolia, dirt doors on huts in Africa, ornate Mexican/Spanish doors — fast sizzle reel of doors from nations we serve
- People **traveling from different places toward a door** — cinematic imagery, overcoming obstacles, arriving to knock
- The act of knocking = perseverance and persistence made visual

---

## Parable Connection

- The parable of the **unjust judge and the persistent widow** — she knocks and knocks until the door opens
- Perseverance = knocking on a door even when it's not opening — *until it opens*
- Tie this to the broader theme: we persevered in prayer, giving, and sowing — God opened the door

---

## Nations / Perseverance Examples

- Nations known for perseverance to potentially feature: **Iran**, Cuba, the Maasai (northern Tanzania), Somali refugees in Ethiopia/Kenya
- The **Messenger X app** opening doors to closed nations (Saudi Arabia, North Korea) — doors the app keeps open that would otherwise be locked

---

## Closing Charge

- Lisa's suggested closing line: **"Tonight we knock on the doors of the nations"**
- Bring the room into the mission together — this is what we're doing tonight

---

## Tone & Feel

- **Simple and clear** — don't overcomplicate the displays or story
- **Urgency**: the door is open but can shut; "tonight we walk through before they shut"
- **Cinematic and impactful** — gut-punch feeling, not abstract
- End with footage of people raising their hands/responding, underscored by the knocking/door theme
