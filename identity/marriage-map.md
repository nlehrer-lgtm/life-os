## The Big Picture

### Where you fit together perfectly

- **Her Discernment is your Frustration.** She is *literally wired* to do the gut-checking work that drains you. Treat her gut read on people, situations, and decisions as the gift it is.
- **You both have Enablement.** Rare and beautiful. Your home is a place where both adults are wired to serve. Your friends and family receive from a household with two givers.
- **Her grounded 6 + your dreamy 9-with-ADHD = balance.** She keeps the foundation solid while you bring the creativity, music, and play. Neither alone would be the home you're building together.

### Where you share gaps

- **Neither of you has Galvanizing.** You don't naturally mobilize crowds. When something needs a whole community of action, ask a friend wired to rally people — don't try to be that yourselves.
- **Neither of you has Wonder.** You're both action-and-care people, not "sit and ask the deeper question" people. You'll rarely stop and ask, *"Are we becoming who we want to be?"* That has to be built in on purpose — Sabbath, retreats, mentors, journaling.
- **Together you're low on Tenacity** (competency for you, frustration for her). Projects pile up half-done. Not a moral failure; it's wiring. Outsource Tenacity for things that *must* finish.

### The dance to watch

- **You both deflect to the other's wants.** Two Enablers + your 9 + her 6 = constant "whatever you want, babe." This leads to doing things neither of you actually wanted. Solution: each of you names what you actually need *first,* before deferring.
- **You can over-defer your opinion.** Your low Discernment + 9's merging means it's easy to lose track of *your own gut.* [[Eliana Lehrer|Ellie]]'s wisdom is a gift, not a replacement for your voice. She wants to hear what *you* actually think too.
- **Her anxiety + your withdrawal is the classic 6/9 conflict spiral.** When she gets vigilant or worried, your instinct is to go quiet. That feels like rejection to a 6. Show up — words and touch matter most when she's stressed.

## Three Scenarios That Capture You

### 1. A Tuesday Afternoon — The Small Idea

Tuesday, mid-afternoon. You're in the kitchen. *"Hey — I was thinking I might run to Home Depot in a bit. I want to grab the brackets for that wall hanging in the living room. Figured I could get it up tonight."*

[[Eliana Lehrer|Ellie]] looks up. *"Yeah, do it. What size brackets are you getting?"*

You pause. *"I was gonna look when I got there."*

*"Babe — just measure first. Last time you came home with two different sizes and had to go back."*

You laugh. *"True."* You measure. You go.

**Underneath:** This is the real version of you two. You're not pitching three-act adventures — your Invention shows up as small concrete moves. [[Eliana Lehrer|Ellie]]'s Discernment doesn't veto you; it sharpens you. Five seconds of her gut-check saves a wasted trip. As a 9, you might feel a flicker of *"I had it"* — but you let it land because she's almost always right. As a 6, the fact that she pushes back at all *is* love. **The marriage isn't sweeping decisions. It's hundreds of small handoffs a week.**

### 2. Friday Night — The Energy Mismatch

Friday, 6:30 PM. The week was long. Shep didn't nap well. [[Eliana Lehrer|Ellie]] just got a text — *"We're having people over tonight if y'all want to come."*

She turns to you. You can already feel your shoulders. *"Do you want to go?"*

She lights up a little. *"I kind of do. It's been a week."*

Pause. Both of you are reading the other one's face. She, the 6, is scanning for what you actually want. You, the 9, are already starting to merge — *whatever you want, babe.* Neither of you is going to push hard for what you actually need.

**Underneath:** This is the most predictable trap in your marriage. You're both Enablers AND her 6 + your 9 makes you both default to the other's preference. You'll often end up doing what *neither* of you wanted.

The honest truth: she's extroverted; she refills *with* people. You're introverted; you refill *away* from them. **Both needs are valid; neither is selfish.**

Real move: each of you says what you actually want *first,* before deferring. *"I'd love a quiet night."* / *"I really need people tonight."* Then negotiate from honesty. Sometimes she goes for an hour while you hold the fort. Sometimes you push out and recharge later. Both beat the resentful *"fine, we'll go."*

### 3. The Loud Kitchen — The Handoff That Just Works

Friday evening. Shep is melting down. [[Saylor Lehrer|Saylor]] is fussing. The kitchen is loud. [[Eliana Lehrer|Ellie]] is two minutes from her own edge — you can see it before she says it.

Your Enablement instinct kicks in: you scoop [[Saylor Lehrer|Saylor]], redirect Shep with something small, slide a glass of water in front of [[Eliana Lehrer|Ellie]] without being asked, take the next ten minutes off her plate.

She exhales. *"Thank you."*

Five minutes later, when things have settled, she's the one who names what was actually happening. *"He's exhausted. He didn't get a real nap. We need to put him down early — not push through dinner."* Your gut wouldn't have called that. Hers did instantly.

**Underneath:**
- **You read her** (Enablement + 9's peace radar). You feel her tension before she names it.
- **She reads the kids** (Discernment + 6's vigilance). She catches the *why* before anyone else.
- Your introversion keeps you steady when the kitchen is loud.
- Her 6 + extroversion tracks everyone in the room at once — superpower *and* exhausting.

The rhythm: **you bring the calm, she brings the read, decisions get made together.** When you try to soothe everything yourself without her input, you may miss what the kids actually need that day.

## Love Languages — How to Actually Fill Each Other's Tank

### Loving Ellie (Quality Time + Gifts)

- **Quality time = phones down, eye contact, undivided attention.** This is hard for you with ADHD tendencies. *Distracted presence doesn't count as quality time to her.* A focused 20 minutes beats a distracted two hours.
- **Thoughtful, specific gifts.** Not generic. Not last-minute. Even small ones. The point is "I was thinking about *you* — I saw *you.*"
- **Watch out for:** drifting into your phone or a project when you're "together." She reads that as not being chosen.

### Loving Nathaniel (Words of Affirmation + Physical Touch)

- **Words of affirmation:** specific, sincere, regular. Tell him what he's doing well — as a husband, as a dad, as a creative. Don't assume he knows.
- **Physical touch:** hand on his back, sitting close on the couch, a real hug. As a 9 introvert, he may not always ask, but it's how he feels home.
- **Watch out for:** [[Eliana Lehrer|Ellie]]'s 6 vigilance can spike when stressed — when that happens, she may unintentionally critique more than affirm. Notice when that's happening and ask for what you need.

## Specific Marriage Realities

### His ADHD + Her Groundedness

You're easily distracted, quirky, creative, and your brain runs in many directions. She's anchored, present, and steady. **This is not a bug; it's the design.** She's the keel; you're the sail. Without her, you'd drift. Without you, life would be safer but less alive.

The risk: she does a lot of invisible holding-it-together work (remembering schedules, tracking what needs attention, noticing what's slipping). Some of that comes from her D + 6 wiring; some of it is real labor that should be named, thanked, and shared.

### His Creativity + Her Non-Creative Wiring

Music, storytelling, projects — these fill *your* soul. They are not how [[Eliana Lehrer|Ellie]] refills. **Don't expect her to be your creative collaborator on the things you make.** She can be your wise sounding board (D), your biggest supporter (E), and your honest gut-check. But she's not going to dream up the next track with you. That's okay — find creative collaborators outside the marriage for the *making,* and bring [[Eliana Lehrer|Ellie]] the *deciding.*

### Her Faith and the 6 Loyalty

A 6 lives or dies by what they trust. Her trust in God, in you, and in the marriage is the bedrock she stands on. Protect that trust like it's your job — because it is. Be consistent. Be honest. Don't surprise her with major decisions; bring her in early.

## When Things Get Hard — The 9/6 Conflict Pattern

The most predictable conflict pattern in your marriage:

1. **Stress hits.** Money, kids, work, something unresolved.
2. **Her 6 vigilance spikes.** She gets anxious, asks lots of questions, may sound critical or pressing.
3. **Your 9 withdraws.** You go quiet, internal, "let me think about it." You're not rejecting her; you're conflict-avoiding.
4. **She reads withdrawal as abandonment.** Her 6 fear is being without support. Your silence triggers it.
5. **It escalates.** Her pressure increases; your withdrawal deepens.

**The break-the-pattern move (yours):** when you feel yourself going quiet, *say so.* *"I'm not going anywhere — I just need ten minutes to think and I'll come back."* That sentence prevents the spiral. Words + a hand on her shoulder before you walk away. That's all she needs.

**The break-the-pattern move (hers):** when you feel the pressure rising, name it. *"I'm getting anxious. I need reassurance, not a solution."* That lets him respond with presence instead of trying to fix.

## A Closing Note

You two are wired well for each other. Most of the gaps in one of you are filled by a genius in the other — your Invention + her Discernment, her groundedness + your creativity, her extroversion + your introversion. The places where you're *both* low (Wonder, Tenacity, Galvanizing) are the places to be honest about and to compensate for intentionally.

The thing this whole map is really saying: **you don't need to be everything for each other. You need to know what you actually are, name it honestly, and protect each other's gifts.** The rest is grace.
