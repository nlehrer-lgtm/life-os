God Is Small.

I often look up at the stars and think about how big God must be.
Or I look out of a plane window and wonder why such a massive entity like God, would ever care about all those little neighborhoods and buildings with people inside. 
Then it hit me. 
God isnít big. 
He is the smallest person in the whole wide world. 
I have a feeling that the idea of ØbignessÆ was something our human minds accidentally shaped together. 
The Bible talks about God being magnificent, and omnipresent, but does it actually say God is massive?
When I think about God being massive it definitely makes me think that he is all powerful and knowing. 
It also makes me believe that he is in control. 
However the idea of God being so awesome and big never makes me feel close and loved by him.
Matter of fact the times where I have felt the closest to God, are when it feels like a persons hand on my shoulder or a warm feeling in my soul.
God entered our dimension as the smallest most insignificant person in the whole world, we all know that story.
It makes me wonder why? Why does God care about such detail and intimacy?

However we donít think of ØloveÆ as a massive being hovering in outer space.
We think of love as a force that is spread throughout the earth, hovering close to every life, and limited by nothing. 
Small and gentle. Yet all encompassing.
God is love.

Rabbis were sometimes described as teaching or praying under fig trees. So this wasnít just shadeîit likely marked a private, spiritually significant moment.

B. Jesus claims prior, personal knowledge

Jesus says:

ØBefore Philip called youèÆ

That rules out:
	Ë	Coincidence
	Ë	Philip telling Jesus about him
	Ë	Public reputation

Whatever happened under that fig tree was:
	Ë	Unobserved by others
	Ë	Known only to Nathanael and God

So when Jesus says, ØI saw you,Æ Nathanael hears:

ØGod saw me.Æ

That explains why he doesnít ask, ØWho told you about me?Æ
He already knows the answer.

?

3. Nathanael was likely in a moment of honest searching

Earlier, Nathanael is skeptical:

ØCan anything good come out of Nazareth?Æ (1:46)

This isnít cynicismîitís scriptural realism. Nazareth isnít in the messianic prophecies. Nathanael appears to be:
	Ë	Biblically literate
	Ë	Spiritually sincere
	Ë	Waiting for God to act, but unwilling to fake belief

That matches Jesusí assessment: no deceit.

So under the fig tree, Nathanael may well have been:
	Ë	Praying for Israelís redemption
	Ë	Wrestling with messianic expectation
	Ë	Asking God for truth, not appearances

When Jesus names him accurately and reveals divine knowledge, Nathanael experiences an answer to a prayer before he speaks it aloud.

?

4. Why the leap to ØSon of GodÆ?

ØRabbi, you are the Son of God; you are the King of Israel.Æ (1:49)

This isnít a fully developed Nicene Christology yet. In first-century Jewish terms:
	Ë	ØSon of GodÆ = Godís anointed king (Psalm 2)
	Ë	ØKing of IsraelÆ = the Messiah

Nathanael is saying:

ØYou are the one Israel has been waiting forîand you know me the way only God does.Æ

Jesus then raises the stakes:

ØYou will see greater things than theseè angels ascending and descending on the Son of Man.Æ (1:50Å51)

Thatís a direct reference to Jacobís ladder (Gen 28)îwhich loops back to the Øno deceit / true IsraelÆ theme. Jesus is claiming to be the meeting place between heaven and earth.

?

5. Why this convinces Nathanael (and would not convince most people)

Youíre right: most people would shrug this off.

But Nathanael isnít most people. He is:
	Ë	Scripturally grounded
	Ë	Spiritually transparent
	Ë	Already seeking God honestly

For someone like that, the combination of:
	1.	Accurate spiritual discernment
	2.	Divine knowledge of a hidden moment
	3.	Fulfillment of Israelís story

doesnít feel like a parlor trickîit feels like recognition.

Not ØYou impressed me,Æ
but ØYou named meîand only God does that.Æ

?

Bottom line

Nathanael doesnít believe because Jesus knew a fact.
He believes because Jesus saw himîin the deepest biblical sense of the word.

If you want, we can also look at:
	Ë	Why John pairs Nathanael with Jacob imagery so tightly
	Ë	Whether Nathanael = Bartholomew in the Synoptics
	Ë	Or how this scene sets the tone for Johnís entire Gospel

Just say the word.
