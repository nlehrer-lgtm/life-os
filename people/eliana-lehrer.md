She doesn't enjoy dreaming up fresh ideas or grinding through obstacles — she enjoys sensing what's true and meeting what's needed.

### Enneagram — 6 (The Loyalist), Extroverted

- **Vigilance & scanning** — naturally anticipates what could go wrong; this powers her Discernment.
- **Fierce loyalty** — once you're her people, she'll go to the wall for you.
- **Security-seeking** — needs to feel that the foundation is solid; can spiral with worry when it isn't.
- **Extroverted** — refills with people. Friend time, hosting, conversation = fuel.

### Personality

- **Grounded** — not floaty, not in her head, not chasing the next shiny thing. Present.
- **Not particularly creative** — doesn't naturally generate ideas, write, make music, or invent. That's not her gift, and that's fine.

### Love Languages

1. **Quality time** — undistracted presence matters more than gifts or words.
2. **Gifts** — thoughtful, specific gifts (especially ones that show *you saw her*) land deeply.

## How to Love and Serve Her Well

- **Bring her your ideas early.** Her Discernment is energizing for her *and* covers your blind spot. Asking her gut read is honoring her gift, not bothering her.
- **Plan quality time intentionally.** Phones down. Eye contact. Don't multitask. A 20-minute conversation where she has your full attention beats a two-hour distracted hang.
- **Give specific, thoughtful gifts** that say "I was thinking about *you*." Not generic. Not last-minute. Even small.
- **Honor her need for people.** As an extrovert, social time refills her — don't read her wanting people as her not wanting *you.*
- **Notice when she's pouring out without being filled.** As a fellow Enabler, both of you can give until you're empty. Show up for her the way she shows up for everyone else.
- **When she's anxious (6 spiral), bring calm.** Don't dismiss her concern; acknowledge it, then bring presence and steadiness.
- **Don't expect her to generate ideas or grind through projects.** Those aren't her gifts. Let her shine where she actually shines.
