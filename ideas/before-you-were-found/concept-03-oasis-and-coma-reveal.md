# Concept 03 — The Oasis, Striving vs. Looking, and the Coma Reveal

> The insight that locked the ending in place: the **bronze serpent** parallel, the truth that you don't *strive* your way free, and the **coma reveal** that recontextualizes the whole story. See [overview.md](overview.md). Pillars: [[Faith]].

---

## The Spark: The Bronze Serpent

This idea came out of reading about how God came down and died for our sins — a parallel to when **Moses lifted up the bronze serpent** for the people of Israel (Numbers 21). All they had to do was **look** at the serpent to be healed. A simple act of faith. It signified that there was nothing they could do except submit and look to God.

That directly foreshadows what Jesus did: **we are all sinners bitten by sin, and the only remedy is looking toward God** (John 3:14–15).

## How It Maps To The Story

The character spends the book *striving, striving, striving* to overcome his sin. But what he finds at the end is that the **little oasis he first woke up by** — the one with the tree, the water, the food, and safety — was what he was supposed to look for the entire time.

He keeps venturing out, looking for ways to escape the wilderness he thinks he's trapped in. The turn comes the moment he **simply sits, takes in the oasis** — not a literal desert oasis, but the true *source of life*, a pool of living water in the middle of a desert — and **embraces that this is where he needs to stay**, where he needs to put his effort and his gaze.

> The oasis is not a waystation. It is the **destination.** He was looking everywhere for relief except toward the source of it.

## The Ending: The Coma Reveal

The moment he embraces the oasis, he **closes his eyes, opens them** — and he's in a room he recognizes in the **real world.**

- The room has **younger pictures of him** from a few years back.
- He's sitting in a chair for disabled people. He can barely move his arms; he can only think and see.
- He has been in a **coma** — living in his imagination for the last few years — while caretakers and his family fed him, prayed for him, and were there for him.
- He was put into the coma through a tragic situation: a car accident or a blunt hit to the head.

The realization: **striving for freedom, striving for the answer, gets him nowhere — but simply recognizing that God is there is the answer.** It's a paradigm shift for how he chooses to live the rest of his life on the real earth.

*(Open: maybe he's not fully disabled after a while; maybe he gains his life back. The degree of recovery is undecided — and may itself be part of the message.)*

## The Underlying Theme To Land

The whole dream and its purpose was for **God to show him** that the sin he was trapped in can only be beaten by **living close to the source of life** — not by running from the sin and hunting for a solution.

## The Sexual-Sin Layer (Keep It Subtle)

- Be **very subtle** with the fact that the sin was sexual — something like cheating on his wife, and how that broke his family.
- After the coma, maybe it has literally been **five years** and his wife has moved on / remarried.
- A redemptive scene at the end: his wife and son come to see him. The wife, who had taken full custody, allows him **back into his son's life** (the son carries the generational weight of the theme).
- A **mutual understanding**: in the five years, both of them have had a work done in their lives. The husband understands that she *needed* to leave — and there's redemption there, not bitterness.
