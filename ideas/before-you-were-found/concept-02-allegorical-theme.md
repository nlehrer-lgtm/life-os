# Concept 02 — The Allegorical Theme & Scope

> The full statement of *what this book is meant to do* — the themes, the influences, and the emotional aim. Everything here is "ideas on the wall," none final, but it gives the general scope. See [overview.md](overview.md). Pillars: [[Faith]], [[Joy & Rest]].

---

## The Pitch

A book that tells an **adventurous story** of a man who gets to live in a physical world that has *physical representations* of sin, love, intimacy, and the invisible attributes of God made real.

In this world, the man represents Nathaniel. The story is about him trying to figure out where he is — and realizing he is living the story of his own life and his own struggles. Specifically: **the struggle that cost him his life and his family.** The anti-hero of the story is **sexual sin**, which led to his downfall.

The outcome: the man realizes, in this new world, how to defeat that sin — how to truly conquer it through a relationship with **Jesus and God.**

## What the Book Should Wrestle With

- The **cost** the struggle has on those around you — your wife, your kids — when you battle something like this.
- The cost on **how you see yourself**, how you view reality, and what you're able and unable to conquer.
- The truth that you don't beat sin by chasing the cure. You beat it by **falling in love with God** more than you love your sin — *"Not just commitment, not just passion, not just intimacy, but all three combined into one."* (The danger is swinging the pendulum too far toward only one of the three.)

## The Feel & Influences

A **C.S. Lewis**–type adventure. The same family as:
- *The Lord of the Rings*
- *The Chronicles of Narnia*
- *The Great Divorce*
- *The Alchemist* — a massive adventure that ends in a very simple truth.

## Themes & "Physical" Motifs To Tackle

This world should represent invisible truths in physical ways:

- **Different dimensions** and how reality is layered.
- **Light** — how light works, the speed of light, the purpose of light, what light *does*. Where there is light, there must be a **shadow**. The parallel between staying in the dark and coming into the light.
- **Sin removed "as far as the east is from the west"** (Psalm 103) made physical/spatial.
- **Reflection / the distorted self** — the man seeing his face in water or glass and seeing only a **distorted** version of himself. At the end, finally seeing a **clear** picture of who he is.
- **Seeing himself from a different point in time** — encountering an earlier version of himself.
- **David & Bathsheba** — the **ewe-lamb** parable, Nathan's prophecy, and the outcome of David's son having to die. Themes to draw from about hidden sin and its cost on a family.
- **The oasis as the source of life** — represented physically (a spring, a tree, an oasis in a great field) as the place to *stay near* rather than escape.

## The Aim

A book that could be **life-changing** when someone reads it — one that gives them the feel of adventure (Tolkien, Lewis, Coelho) and leaves them with **hope, strength, and passion to beat the sin in their life.**

And the way they beat it, the way they overcome it, is by **living every day near the source of life** — a passionate, intimate, committed relationship with God. At the end of the day, it all comes down to **loving God more than you love your sin.**
