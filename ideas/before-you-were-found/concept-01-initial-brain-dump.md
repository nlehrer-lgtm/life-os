# Concept 01 — Initial Brain Dump (The First Spark)

> The original prayer-closet vision — the very first time the idea came. Preserved as a raw voice transcript, lightly cleaned for readability but kept faithful to the original. Nathaniel's note at the time: *"I'm only copying this here for the purpose of saving some of these ideas. I'm not married to the current idea for this story."* This is a concept archive, not a commitment to a final direction. See [overview.md](overview.md). Pillars: [[Faith]].

## The Vision (raw)

I was sitting here in my prayer closet, and I had an idea, a vision. I don't know if this vision is just for me, or if it's a story — if it's an adventure — but I'm going to talk about what I've seen.

It started with me standing in the middle of a field. Nothing else was around me. It stemmed from the idea where Jesus says that a man must lose everything to save his soul — *what good is it for him to gain the world and lose himself?* It's the idea of a man standing alone in a field, and everywhere around him is just open field. The sun is shining. He's steady, he's standing. He walks. And there's a wall.

It's a **red wall**. It goes as high as the eye can see and as far to the left and right as well. It's made of some sort of glass — thick, almost like ice, but it's not cold.

After wandering around — well, even before that — the man remembers that he had woken up on the sand/grass of a little bubbling brook, a little pond. Almost like a well that has been welling up. It's a small little watering hole. There are a few bushes, rocks, and an apple tree right near there. Actually, a **fig tree**.

The man journeys around all day, looking for something, but there's just nothing in all directions. He journeys far enough to go to one side and finds that it **drops off into nothingness**. He gets lost and scared. He gets thirsty and hot. He barely has any energy. But he barely sees the fig tree in the distance. He goes running back, back. He's so thankful when he gets back to have water to drink and figs to eat. He goes to sleep underneath the tree — and then he hears a sound, but he doesn't know what it is.

He wakes up the next morning to go explore again. This time, he comes across the same thing. He went in a different direction — what he believes to be the direction the sun rose. He finds another drop-off into nothingness. He's distraught. But he heads straight back, has more water and figs, and goes to bed. That night he hears another noise.

The next day he determines to go west — the direction the sun has risen. He walks as far as he can, and he finally sees the red wall. He can't see through it. He starts to walk along it, looking for a way around. He doesn't know what's on the other side. He keeps **flashing back to his life** this whole time — his family, his marriage. He's failing as a husband and a father. The **sexual sin** contaminates his entire family.

He tries to break the wall. When he comes to, he realizes he had fallen asleep. It's almost night. He doesn't have time to make it back. He has a terrible night's sleep — so scared, so lonely, so lost. He's scared of his own face in the reflection of the red wall.

When he wakes up, he's back by the water. He doesn't remember how he got there. The next day, he gets a rock and goes and tries to break the red wall. He does — it cracks. Covered in sweat and tears, he breaks through. He stumbles through the opening. **It's exactly like it is on the other side.** When he turns around, the wall is completely back together. He starts screaming and yelling. He goes back and bangs on the wall with his fists.

Now he's scared, because he knows he doesn't have the water and the figs. He starts sprinting to where the water and figs would have been on the other side — which means continuing to head west. He goes and goes and goes. As he continues to run, he comes across what looks like a tree in the distance. It's getting late, almost dark. As he comes closer, he sees barely through the distance — **a man sleeping under the tree.** He stops in his tracks. Somebody else has found his mirage.

*(Half-formed at the time, this knits into:)* the **mirror** passage — anyone who listens to the word but does not do what it says is like a man who looks at his face in a mirror and, after doing so, goes away and immediately forgets what he looks like. And the **new creation** — the old is gone; the only way to get rid of the old is to become a new creation.

He sees the man sleeping and he's terrified. He doesn't have anything to defend himself with. He doesn't know if he should go wake the man or hide. He decides to walk miles away, where he can lie in the grass and spy on the man. The next day, he sees the man wake up, and he watches the man leave.

*(Recurring fragment running underneath it all:)* **As far as the east is from the west** — that's how far He has removed our sin from us; a distance that never ends.

## Verses That Came to Mind

- **Ephesians** — "put off your old self."
- **2 Corinthians 5:17** — "the old is gone, the new has come."
- **Psalm 103:12** — "as far as the east is from the west, so far has He removed our transgressions from us."
- **James 1:23–24** — "he who does not obey God's commands is like a man who looks in a mirror, but immediately forgets what he looks like."
